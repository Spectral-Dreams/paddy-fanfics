Chapter 35: Inhumanity Required
And the sun's all up in the sky. The Kindred snooze away, their cold, icy bodies going through a process of rejuvenation. The world outside is bathed in the sun's golden light.
The mortals ready themselves- physically and mentally- for the day ahead. Work, school, university, the florists, the beauty salon, wherever and whatever else.
The sun-scorched streets are abuzz with activity. The sidewalks are not so empty, either. Cars, as usual, clog up the roads. Honking horns and choice words fill the early morning air.
The unremarkable SAD HQ is also abuzz with activity, but of a very different kind. After last night's mess-up, Special Agent Cuomo is really trying to figure out a way to salvage this op. His subordinate, Agent Heartley, is doing the same.
The big man blows a tired raspberry and spins on his chair, "Ughhhh...so, any ideas yet?"
The early morning sun peeks through the blinds of the office windows. Agent Heartley gets off the phone with someone, her expression grim and her tone somber. She looks over at her boss and says, "Uh...nope. But we're in for a rough time."
Special Agent Cuomo groans and rubs his forehead, "Oh, great. Tell me something that I don't know--"
As he's wallowing in his own misery, a knock on the door interrupts him. The door opens and a man in a suit walks in. He places some sort of manilla folder on the desk and explains, "Your CI, sir. Mr. X. He had someone send this. For your eyes only."
Agent Heartley's eyes widen and she exclaims, "What?! Did--did you apprehend the person who sent this?"
The suit guy shakes his head, "No, we did not. He said he worked for Mr. X, not that we had any reason to doubt him. The package was thoroughly checked and scanned, so it's safe to say that there's nothing nasty inside."
Special Agent Cuomo tears the folder open and finds a few photographs inside. He takes it out and examines it closely, "What the hell is this?"
Suit man takes his leave and closes the door with a slight thud. Agent Heartley peers over her superior's shoulder and sees the photos, "Who...is that...guy?"
Special Agent Cuomo mumbles, "Looks like we have an address, basic info, everything. Oh, wait...here, read this."
Agent Heartley sees a small, handwritten note and reads it aloud, "'He's a vamp sympathizer. I'm sure that you'll handle the rest. More info this evening- this is gonna be a major one. Love, Mister X.'"
Special Agent Cuomo inspects the intel again, "So, we got some guy named...Farooq. He's got a tech store in this city. Blank body sympathizer, huh? Can't say that I'm surprised. Heather? You want point on this?"
Agent Heartley declines, "I'm more interested in what our CI has for us in the evening, Harold. Why don't you take point on this one? Besides, I have a meeting with the tech guys in a few minutes. They're supposed to give me an update on the laptop."
Special Agent Cuomo downs the last of his coffee (with a tiny bit of booze mixed in), wipes his mouth, and stands up, "Alright. I'll check this guy out and see what he knows."
Agent Heartley grins fiendishly, "Is that all you're gonna do?"
Special Agent Cuomo puts on his FBI cap, "Oh, you know me, Heather...I like to get creative when out on the field. Contrary to what a bunch of bleeding hearts say, enhanced interrogation techniques work wonders." There is an eerily disturbing glint in his eyes.
Agent Heartley nods along, "Just...just don't go overboard, OK?"
Special Agent Cuomo snickers, "When it comes to keeping Americans safe, there's no such thing as going overboard. You'd do well to keep that in mind, Heather."
He then leaves the office and heads for the elevator. The manilla folder and its contents remain splayed across the desk. Agent Heartley takes another look at the photos and the note.
She bites her lower lip and sighs, "Hmm...let's hope that Harry's CI has something juicy for ME to tackle."
Special Agent Cuomo gathers a few SAD operatives and hops into an SUV- they're all briefed on the way to Farooq's tech store.
"Alright, folks! Listen up! We just received some fresh intel on a possible blank body sympathizer. We're gonna go in there, bust the doors down, and...have a nice little chat with him."
The SUV speeds down the road and stops right outside the store. Special Agent Cuomo and his team storm inside the store. A few thugs go around and block off the exits.
Farooq, the store owner, is caught completely off guard. He yelps and drops the box he was carrying. His eyes widen to an alarming degree as he is approached by 2 large men.
Special Agent Cuomo pulls out his pistol, points it at him, and says, "You! On the floor, now!"
Farooq shakily complies and lies belly-down onto the floor. Special Agent Cuomo squats next to him and lowers his voice, "You're gonna tell us everything you know, buddy."
Farooq fearfully stammers, "My--my--my papers are in order! I am a legal--"
Special Agent Cuomo whacks the poor guy with the underside of his pistol, "We ain't interested in that, pal. We wanna know about the blank bodies."
Farooq is visibly confused, "The--the what?"
The head of the SAD operation growls and violently hoists the quivering man up to his feet, "Don't play dumb with me, asshole! The blank bodies! You know, those freaks that look like they just crawled out of a goddamn grave?!"
Farooq shakes his head and tries to wiggle free of the 2 men who are tightly gripping him, "I--I--I don't know what you're talking about! Please, I beg of you, let me go! I am an--an honest citizen, I swear to--"
Special Agent Cuomo smacks him in the face, "Shut up! You're gonna tell me everything you know, or I'll blow your brains out! And I'll find your fuckin' family and any friends you have and do the same thing to them! You hear me?!"
Farooq is now crying. Special Agent Cuomo delivers another savage punch. The last thing Farooq sees before blacking out is the blurry image of a fist heading towards his face. He slumps over and passes out.
A little later...
The store has been completely closed down and sealed off by the SAD goons. Farooq is bound to a chair somewhere in the grimy backroom of the store. Special Agent Cuomo is standing in front of him, arms crossed.
Farooq blinks a few times and groans as he can feel a sharp pain in his jaw area. His vision is still blurry, but he can make out the vague shape of someone standing in front of him.
Special Agent Cuomo looks down on the poor man contemptuously, "You're not a bloodsucker- we already checked. So...Farooq or whatever the fuck your name is. After your little nap, are you in a...talking mood?"
"I swear! I do not know what you are talking about! PLEASE! This is madness, insanity! Walla, I--"
Special Agent Cuomo hits him again, "Quit your jibbering, sand monkey! Don't give me that shit! You're gonna tell me everything you know about the blank bodies, or I'll make you wish you were never born!"
Farooq mutters, "You--you have no right to--to talk to people like that. I am an honest, tax-paying--"
Special Agent Cuomo grits his teeth and jabs the barrel of his pistol against the man's forehead, "I said SHUT UP! You're gonna talk! YOU'RE GONNA TELL ME WHAT I WANNA HEAR or you're gonna die! Do you understand that, huh? DO YOU?!"
Farooq's lower lip quivers while a few droplets of blood trickle down his nose. Special Agent Cuomo cocks the hammer of his pistol and growls, "Talk. I want names, numbers, addresses, descriptions."
Farooq insists, "I do not know what--what these blank err...bodies are. If--if I did, then I would absolutely be calling the authorities. I am not that kind of person who hides things from the government. I like--love this country, sir."
Special Agent Cuomo presses the barrel harder against Farooq's forehead, "Bullshit! All you fuckin' Jihadis are the same! You all want to destroy America! You wanna see it burn, don't you?! Is that why you got into bed with vampires, huh?!"
Farooq stammers and looks at the fuming man and gasps, "V--vampires? Walla, no! I--I know absolutely nothing of such things! I swear to you, sir, on my life, on my family's life, on my ancestors' graves also! I--I am but a humble shopkeeper, sir!"
Special Agent Cuomo grits his teeth and thunders, "Goddamn terrorist! YOU KNOW something! And I'll do everything that's within my power to pry it outta your stubborn ass!"
Farooq starts bawling and pleads, "Please! I beg of you, sir! I know nothing of--of--of these sorts of things! I keep my head down, mind my own business, and peacefully attend to my affairs! Please, sir! I am begging you!"
Special Agent Cuomo lowers his pistol and scoffs, "Tch."
He rudely asks a few of his fellow goons, "Did you find anything yet?!"
An agent steps forward and hands her boss a list of items, "Yeah. A whole lotta shit. He's got some weird-ass stuff here, sir."
Special Agent Cuomo perches an eyebrow and mumbles to himself the contents of the clipboard's list. He sees a bunch of electronics, computer parts, other techy stuff. Nothing out of the ordinary.
However, his grimace slowly turns into a wicked grin as he sees a particular item on the list: burner phones. A lot of burner phones.
"Bingo."
Farooq's breathing is heavy and his shoulders rise and fall rapidly. Special Agent Cuomo hands the clipboard back to the agent and snickers, "You know, I've been doing this shit for quite a while now...I know all the ways in which vampires hide their activities."
He squats in front of Farooq and continues, "And, if you think that I'm gonna let you get away with this, then you are sorely mistaken, buddy. You've been getting bulk orders for a ton of burner phones. I want a name. Now."
Farooq whimpers and gulps, "I--I have no ide--idea what you are talking about."
Special Agent Cuomo chuckles mirthlessly, "Don't play games with me, Jihadi. I want names. You've been getting an unusually large amount of burner phones. That's not normal. It's suspicious. And, if you're smart, you'll give me what I want."
Farooq's eyes dart from side to side, "This--in case--in case you have noticed, this is a tech store. I--I keep burner phones in stock. I do not ask why my customers want them. I just sell them. That is all."
Special Agent Cuomo clicks his tongue derisively, "You see, I don't believe you. And after what you people did, I have absolutely no reason to trust you."
Farooq protests, "But sir! I had NOTHING to do with what you--you are referring to. Walla, I was sick on that tragic and--and horrible day. But I--I saw it on the news, sir. And I--I cried. I mourned the many losses, I swear! I--I am not like any of those--those monsters, sir."
Special Agent Cuomo holsters his pistol and sighs, "That's what they always say..." he trails off and begins to slowly walk around the grimy backroom. A sickly white, fluorescent light provides a good deal of illumination.
The FBI agent looks over at the terrified man and asks, "So...electronics, bootleg CDs, used laptops, burner phones, and other such shit. Tell me, how does one get into this kind of business?"
Farooq swallows the lump in his throat, "D--don't you people know everything? I--I mean, aren't you supposed to know it all?"
"Don't play coy with me. Remember: I'm not the one tied to a fuckin' chair."
Farooq winces as he can feel his bonds digging into his wrists and ankles, "Ow. Ouch. I--I do not know what you--you want to know. I conduct my business with integrity, honesty, and above all, honor. I am not a liar, sir."
Special Agent Cuomo rolls his eyes, "Yeah, yeah." He peers into a box of DVDs and sees that they are mostly bootleg copies of movies. He picks up a copy of the latest blockbuster movie and chuckles, "Hmm...bootlegging, huh?"
Farooq lowers his head a little, "Times are...tough, s--sir. Besides, everyone else on this street was doing it."
Special Agent Cuomo shakes his head and continues to ominously pace around, "Huh. Everybody else was doing it, huh? Yeah, that's what they all say. I've heard that line so many times, it makes me sick."
He stops and takes a good look around, "Sometimes, you don't have a cattle prod or a pair of pliers lying around, so...you gotta get creative."
His eyes immediately flit over to an open toolbox. He saunters over to it and sees that there are several tools inside. A screwdriver, a wrench, a hammer, etc. A few large nails, too.
Farooq's eyes widen and he screams, "NO! NO! PLEASE!" he tries to squirm out of his bonds, but it's no use. The ropes are tied too tightly.
Special Agent Cuomo picks up a hammer and grins sadistically, "You know, I really hate your kind. What you did was...inexcusable. Besides, I have my orders. By any and all available means necessary. I like a blank check, heh."
Farooq pleads, "Please! No!"
With wide eyes he looks at the other thugs in the room and desperately begs, "Help! Please! He's going to hurt me! Help!"
1 of the agents steps forward and holds him down, "Sorry, buddy. We're not here to help you. We're here to get some answers outta you."
Farooq starts crying again and babbles incoherently, "Please! PLEASE! NO!"
Special Agent Cuomo retrieves some nails and nods over to a few agents. They firmly nod back and drag the chair that Farooq is bound to over to a nearby table.
This table is a wooden table with a few rusty metal legs. The top of the table is littered with various magazines, electronic components, wires, parts, and the like.
Farooq continues to scream and beg for mercy. An agent viciously wipes the table clean with a swipe of the hand. Special Agent Cuomo then slams the first nail onto the table and gives Farooq a nasty grin.
"Tell me what I wanna know. Last chance..."
Farooq stammers and weakly gasps. Words fail him. Special Agent Cuomo then drives the nail into the poor fella's web space (the gap between the fingers). Farooq screams bloody murder.
The nail sticks out of his hand and blood pools around it. Special Agent Cuomo nods to another agent who then grabs a second nail and hammers it into the other hand.
"AAAAAAAAARGH!" Farooq's scream is bloodcurdling and guttural. His face is a contorted mask of pain. He feels like he's going to pass out.
"Tell me...what I wanna know. Burner phones. Bulk orders. Who?"
Farooq looks at his bloodied hands and sees the 2 nails sticking out of each of them. He then looks up at the man who is asking him these questions.
Special Agent Cuomo callously averts his gaze and jerks his chin to the other hammer-holding agent, "You know what to do."
The agent then proceeds to hammer another nail Farooq's hand webbing. Both hands are done almost simultaneously. Farooq cries out in pain once more.
Special Agent Cuomo repeats, "Tell me what I wanna know. Burner phones. A whole lotta them. Who's been making the bulk orders?"
Farooq's vision is blurry and he can barely make out the shape of the creatures surrounding him. He sways from side to side, but he's held in place by a few other SAD operators who are standing behind him.
"Who? WHO?!"
Farooq is on the verge of passing out.
Special Agent Cuomo pinches the back of his neck and jolts him awake, "Hey! Stay with me, buddy! Don't go passing out on me! You're used to the sight of blood, aren't you?"
Farooq weakly mumbles, "I--I know...nothing. Please, e--end this insanity, I beg of--of you...habibi--"
Special Agent Cuomo has a look of disgust etched across his face, "Tch. I'm gonna ask you 1 last time--"
He gestures to the other hammer-wielder to get another nail ready, "--who ordered those burner phones. I need a name, a description, and...an address, preferably in that order."
Farooq groans and tries to keep his eyes open.
Special Agent Cuomo impatiently growls, "You got 6 seconds before I start driving nails into the rest of your goddamn hands. 5..."
"I know nothing! I swear, if--if I did, I would've already re--reported it to the authorities!!!"
"3...not impressed..."
"You cannot do this to me! I have--"
Special Agent Cuomo sighs, "0. You know what to do..."
2 more nails- 1 for each hand- are hammered into Farooq's webbing. He screams again and starts to sob.
"YAAAAAHRGH! I--I know nothing! Please, at once--stop this madness!"
The blood flows to the edge of the table and drips onto the floor. Special Agent Cuomo shakes his head and says, "You're not cooperating. And I don't like an uncooperative son of a bitch."
Farooq is in such agonizing pain that he struggles to stay conscious. Special Agent Cuomo then nods to the other agents. They grab a hold of the poor guy's arms and stretch them out. A ripping sound is heard as his hands are pulled away from the standing nails.
"AAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAGH!"
Special Agent Cuomo leans in closer to Farooq and lowers his voice to a menacing growl, "Burner phones. Bulk orders. Who's the customer...or customers, hmm?"
Farooq is breathing heavily and his shoulders rise and fall rapidly. He weakly mumbles, "I--I do not know what you are talking about. YOU ARE A--A MO--"
Special Agent Cuomo smacks the back of Farooq's head and tuts, "Oh, the only monster here is you. You know something. And I'm gonna find out what it is."
He then nods to the other agents who reposition Farooq's bleeding hands over the table. The poor guy is still crying and sobbing.
Special Agent Cuomo takes a deep breath and says, "Last chance...or else you won't be able to do a whole lotta things with these mangled hands of yours."
Farooq sniffles and chokes out, "I know nothing! I swear! On all that is humane and--and righteous, I SWEAR!"
Special Agent Cuomo rapidly clicks his tongue, "Tsk, tsk, tsk. Not convincing, not even remotely convincing. And I'm a very impatient man. You got 3 seconds."
Farooq closes his eyes and silently prays for some semblance of strength and mercy.
"1..."
"Please...have mercy on me."
"2..."
"I beg of you!"
"3..." Special Agent Cuomo gives the other hammer-holder the go-ahead. Nail after nail is mercilessly driven into the poor guy's hands. They don't just focus on his webbing like last time; they go for the tips of fingers, the palm, and the knuckles.
The blood flows freely and pools onto the table. Farooq screams in pain and nearly passes out again. He tightly grits his teeth and tries to stay conscious.
Special Agent Cuomo leans in closer and whispers, "Give me a name. Give me a description. Gimme an address, something that I can use. The ball's in your court, buddy. You've got the power to stop this, I don't."
Farooq sniffles and mumbles, "I--I know--please, I--stop this at--at once."
The head honcho nods to the other agents who pull Farooq's arms back. Another tearing sound is heard. Fleshy bits of skin are ripped off.
"AAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAGH!"
"You're not making this any easier for me, huh?" Special Agent Cuomo pulls out his pistol and holds it up against the poor man's temple.
Farooq blinks a few times and tries to catch his breath.
Special Agent Cuomo pulls the hammer back and cocks the gun, "I'm gonna ask you 1 last time: who ordered those damn burner phones? I know that you're connected to the blanks. I know that they've been using you- they do that to people, a lot. Now...who is it?"
Farooq slowly shakes his head and shakily mutters, "I...I...cannot--give--"
Special Agent Cuomo scoffs and looks at his subordinates, "You heard that? He knows, this son of a bitch KNOWS! But he can't give up whoever his vampire pals are, huh?"
Some mutters and groans emanate from the other agents. Special Agent Cuomo then looks at Farooq and growls, "I'm gonna give you 1 last chance. Your hands are done for, so you kiss goodbye to a whole lotta fun stuff. What other body part do you think we should work on next? Hmm?"
Farooq blinks tightly and sniffs loudly, "I...please--"
Special Agent Cuomo screams into the guy's ear, "ANSWER ME!"
Farooq flinches and gasps, "I--I cannot tell you what I do not know!!"
From there, it devolves into more nails being hammered into what remains of Farooq's hands. The poor guy screams in pain and tries to squirm free, but it's of no use.
Hammers send nails in and blood comes spurting out. It's a gruesome sight to behold. Bones break and flesh is torn apart.
Special Agent Cuomo carries out the whole thing with a cold, dead expression. He doesn't feel anything for the shopkeeper.
Farooq is now whimpering and crying uncontrollably. His hands are completely mangled and bloody. The nails stick out of his hands and blood drips onto his shoes. He struggles to keep his eyes open.
Special Agent Cuomo dryly inspects the hammer and sets it aside. He touches a few of the protruding nails and asks, "So...are you ready to talk now, hmm? That's some blood loss you're experiencing right there. You might pass out if you're not careful."
Farooq weakly mumbles, "I--I know...I don--don't know...stop...at once...please, s--sir. I am...I am innocent...man...please."
Special Agent Cuomo scoffs bitterly, "Innocent, huh? None of you people are innocent. Now, the next thing outta that big mouth of yours better be something useful, or else..." he lets the threat hang in the dense air.
Farooq sniffles and weakly says, "I--I know--"
Special Agent Cuomo groans and hammers a nail into Farooq's knee. The poor guy cries out in pain and winces.
"Talk! Tell me what I wanna hear! NAMES! NAMES, DAMMIT!"
Farooq yells out, "Velma! Velma! Velma! H--her name is Velma!"
Special Agent Cuomo stares deeply into the poor man's eyes and growls, "Velma? Is that it? Velma? That's not good enough for me! Gimme a last name! NOW, ASSHOLE! NOW!"
Farooq hesitates and stammers, "I--I--I do not know her last name. She--she never told me."
Special Agent Cuomo ruthlessly orders the other hammer-carrier to do the same thing that was just done, but to the other knee.
"AAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAAGRGRGHH!"
"Talk! Give me more details! LAST NAME! Velma! VELMA WHO, HUH?!"
Farooq screams out, spittle flying everywhere, "DuPont! DUPONT!! She--she always came to my shop and--and bought those burner phones in--in bulk! C--cash only!"
Special Agent Cuomo cracks a smile of sheer satisfaction and nods to his fellow agents, "Good. Good. That's what I like to hear. Velma DuPont, hm? Alright, we'll have to look into whoever this bitch is once we get back."
He ducks till he's level with Farooq's face and asks, "Anything else? Is there anything else that you're withholding from us?"
Farooq can barely answer because of the amount of pain that he's experiencing. He weakly shakes his head and says, "No. No. I--I know nothing else."
Special Agent Cuomo cups the torture victim's cheek and asks, "Are you sure?"
Farooq weakly gulps, "I--I am, y--yes."
"Very well. I believe you." Special Agent Cuomo rises up, pulls out his pistol, aims it at Farooq's head, and fires. Blood and brain matter splatter all over the place.
The bullet makes a small hole in Farooq's forehead, and it causes his head to snap back violently.
None of the other agents so much as object, let alone flinch. They're all too inured to this kind of nasty, nasty shit.
Special Agent Cuomo holsters his pistol and walks over to the door. He sternly orders, "Grab whatever's useful, get rid of the body, and torch the rest. We need to keep sending a message to our vampire friends. A message that we're here...and we don't fuck around."
The other agents nod and carry out their superior's orders without question. They grab some techy stuff, burner phones, laptops, and so on. A few of them even pocket whatever cash (or other stuff) that they can find.
Bastards. The lot of them.
A couple of agents drag Farooq's lifeless body out of the backroom and dump it in the middle of the store. Once the kerosene has been doused all over the place, a match is lit and tossed.
Flames lick the walls and spread throughout the store. The agents quickly leave the premises and hop into their vans. As the vans are screeching away, the fire engulfs the entire store.
It's soon a blazing inferno. Farooq's lifeless body is left inside to burn.

Elsewhere...
