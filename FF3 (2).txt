Chapter 29: Waiting- Part 2
The sun is now high and bright. Whilst the Unliving sleep throughout the whole day, the mortals (and a few Ghouls) are already up and about. Their day is just about to begin.
Cars begin zooming along cracked, potholed streets. Some kids are already out playing with each other or getting ready for school. A few people are outside, dressed in their summery clothes as they partake of the most important meal of the day.
(Or gulping down 1 cup of coffee after another while briskly walking to large office buildings of glass and steel).
There are some early commuters taking the bus or driving their own vehicles to their places of labor. Another day has begun in Flimont City and it's (as usual) pretty damn hot.
It's the sort of hot that makes one feel like the sun has already burnt their skin to a crisp. The kind of harsh white-yellow sunlight that makes everything so glaringly bright.
As the mortal (mostly) residents of the city prepare themselves for yet another day, the immortals are still asleep. Some probably have dreams in their near-Torpid state whilst others float around in a cold, dreamless abyss.
Afternoon fades away with little fanfare, and the setting sun of the impending evening sets the sky ablaze with vibrant, beautiful reds and oranges.
The hot, shimmering haze, however, remains. Once the sun has fully withdrawn from view, the Kindred wake up and emerge from their resting spots.
(Which are generally NOT coffins unless one is a Toreador intending to make some kind of a commentary on a cliché or if they are an Anarch upstart with a semblance of humor).
Nevertheless, Clarice (the Malkavian Primogen) stirs from the throes of the day-sleep. The Neonate awakens, blinking a few times, and she looks around.
Ms. Barton licks her lips and prepares for the night ahead. After bathing, she goes to see how her Ghoul, Arnold, is doing. He's sitting in the living room, hands clasped together, and looking a bit lost.
He's got that sorta vacant, empty (albeit innocent) expression on his face.
Clarice puts her hand on Arnold's shoulder and gently inquires, "How are you this evening, Arnold?"
Arnold nods a few times, "I'm doing great, teacher. Uh...how are you?"
"The night's only beginning, so I'm yet to see how things will go for me." The Malkavian has a wide smile on her lips.
Clarice is dressed in a long polka-dotted dress. Her shoes are a simple pair of flats. Her hair is done up into a simple bun. She's got a few necklaces around neck.
She looks the part of a doting schoolteacher from another time.
Arnold is dressed in faded shorts and a simple green t-shirt with a faded, worn-out logo of some sports team from last century. His socks are mismatched. He looks like someone on a lazy summer vacation...of sorts.
Clarice moves her living room curtain aside and frowns when she spots a jet-black sedan parked across her Haven. The windows are tinted black and the plate looks fake. It's impossible to know if the driver is looking at her or not.
That car was also here last night. Arnold reports, "Um...that car is the same one, right? Maybe somebody mistook this place for free parking?"
He uneasily lets out a few nervous chuckles.
Clarice, for her part, simply shakes her head and sighs, "No, no. Arnold, listen to me..."
Arnold gulps and lowers his head.
The Malk firmly reminds her Ghoul, "These are trying times, my dear. I've gotten involved in something that is simply too complex for you to understand, alright?"
"O--OK. Um, so...what do I have to do?"
"I can't stop you from going out and living your life. But remember what I told you. Can you please recall my words, Arnold?" Clarice's voice is soft and soothing.
"I'll try not to attract too much attention to myself, teacher. And if someone...err...approaches me with bad intent written all over their uh...faces...I should...run. Did I miss anything, teacher?"
Clarice warmly smiles at the Ghoul, "Run...and don't stop. Don't even look back. If you're in danger, I'll know how- and where- to find you. And you do remember all the important phone numbers, yes?"
"Mm-hmm."
Clarice reminds him, "Making phone calls should only be a last resort. And ONLY to the people whose numbers I've had you memorize, OK?"
Arnold nods, "I understand, teacher. Um...so...where will you be going tonight?"
Clarice smooths out the few wrinkles on her dress, "To meet a new friend of mine. It's nothing too big, really."
"Oh, a new friend. Who? Can I also meet 'em?"
Clarice stifles a sigh and warmly smiles, "When the time's right. Right now, Arnold, we have to stay focused on making sure that no...bad people hurt us...or our Clan."
"Oh, yeah, yeah. Absolutely."
"You can never be too careful, dearie. There's yesterday's casserole in the fridge- I hope that's enough for you?"
Arnold smiles broadly, "It is. Thanks."
Clarice softly responds, "Good. Good. I'll be on my way now. Just remember what I've been telling you. And if you are forced to run, don't stop and DON'T look back. Keep running until you're safe; I'll find you, even if it means going to the other side of the country, alright?"
Arnold gulps and slowly nods, "Mm-hmm. I kinda sucked in uh...sports back at school. You remember how the other kids wo--would pick on and tease me when I came last in that running race?"
Clarice cups Arnold's cheek with her cold hand, "Arnold, my dear, that was so long ago. In the here-and-now, I know that you can do what needs to be done. Do you understand how special you are, Arnold? Do you?"
Arnold looks down and remains silent.
Clarice softly continues, "You are very unique in your own ways, Arnold. Never forget that. Now, I have to go. Stay safe and make sure to lock the door behind me, OK?"
Arnold shyly nods his head and does just that as the Malkavian Primogen walks away, her steps echoing in the night. She walks up to the strange sedan parked out front.
She raps her knuckles on the window and signals for whoever's in there to roll down the window. After a few seconds, the window is lowered down. The driver is a tall, broad-shouldered man with spiky red hair and hardened features.
He's wearing a long leather coat and drinking something from a large cup with a transparent plastic straw sticking out.
He grins at Clarice and his voice is low and gruff, "Hello there, miss."
Clarice glowers at him, "Who do you think you are and why have you been parked outside of my house for the past few nights?"
The man brings the drink to his lips and takes a good, long sip, "I think you know who I am and why I'm here."
The Seer holds back a growl, "The Prince sent you, didn't he? What is this supposed to be, hmm? An intimidation tactic?"
"Nope. I'm just here for recon. Orders are to keep a close eye on you...and that meandering little--"
Clarice bares her fangs and grabs this thug by his neck, pulling him in closer, "Listen here, you callous brute. If you so much as lay a finger on my Ghoul, I will see to it that you are torn to shreds and fed to the vermin of this Domain. Am. I. Clear?"
The Prince's goon shoves her hand off of his throat and scoffs, "Crystal. But don't worry, I ain't here for your Ghoul. Orders were to watch all of you after that trick you tried to pull off."
He takes another sip before adding, "And if you try anything stupid, then...he gets it."
Before Clarice can retaliate, he holds out his hand, "Now, now, now...do--don't hit me, lady. You know what happens if you do. I'm just the fucking messenger here, lady. Just the messenger. I don't even know half of what the hell I've been told. And I don't get paid enough to care either way."
Clarice sneers at him, "So, that's what Prince Antonio is having you lackeys do, hmm? Follow us around, watch our every move, and wait for an excuse to come along so he can justify further abusing his power? I must say, that is quite the low."
The thug smirks, "I don't know and I don't care. But I'm gonna tell the boss that you tried to hurt me tonight."
The Malk shakes her head, "Tell that fool whatever you feel like. Since you're following me around like a dog, you might as well know where I'm headed. As a matter of fact, why not give me a lift, hmm? What say you?"
The redheaded man's jaw goes slack, "Wha--? Oh, no. No, no, no. Hell no. You are not riding in the back with me."
Clarice calmly circles around to the door to the passenger seat and opens it, "Oh, but I am. I insist. If you aren't willing to play ball, then...well, I'm not going to lower myself to making such pointless threats."
The goon concedes with a harsh sigh, "Hrrf. Fine. Uhm..."
Clarice grimaces when she sees the scattered food wrappers, crushed cans, and other refuse strewn about the floor of the vehicle.
He grunts, "Just--just toss that stuff in the back, lady. Or wiggle your way around it, alright? And don't look at me like that, OK? I've been following your ass for a while now."
Clarice remarks, "Well, maybe next time you can do a better job at being stealthy...and cleaner. Goodness, it's like the top of an overflowing dumpster in here!"
She gets in and pulls on the seatbelt, "Do you not have some basic sense of self-respect, dignity, and--and hygiene?"
The thug rolls his eyes, "Whatever. Hey, don't touch the radio. That's non-negotiable."
Clarice wiggles around until she's feeling reasonably comfortable.
She leans against the seat, closes her eyes, and breathes a deep, drawn-out sigh, "Alright. I have to feed first. So, drive a little slowly. After that, I want to go to the cemetery nearby."
The man starts up his car, the engine sputters and coughs for a bit, and then he begins driving down the street.
"Thanks for the whole itinerary, lady! The Prince is gonna love this, hahahaha!"
Clarice grimly mumbles, "Or he might very well end up not loving it. In which case, getting...fired would be the least of your problems, I'm afraid."
"Yeah, yeah, whatever. Now, keep a lid on it. And stop staring at me like that, sheesh! It's creepy!"
"Hmm."
After some slow driving through the darkening neighborhood, Clarice asks him to stop right by an old laundromat. The building has long since faded into total obscurity.
The walls are either broken, crumbling, or peeling apart. The windows are cracked and shattered. The doors were shoddily boarded shut. There are a few people milling about here and there.
The man nods over to some random person pacing around and snarks, "Hey, look, it's your dinner. You want me to whack 'em, apply some seasoning, and bring 'em to you on a silver--"
The Seer holds up a stern finger, "That shall not be necessary, thank you. Such brutish methods might be what you and your ilk are usually used to, but they are far beneath me. You'll just have to sit here and wait until I'm done, alright?"
The thug shrugs and mutters, "And if you do fuck things up, I got a few body bags, duct tape, and some really tight ropes. No one would miss your meals, lady. Trust me."
The Malkavian clicks her tongue and gets out of the car. Her surroundings are sparse and nearly barren. A few regular-looking types lolling about an old laundromat that's in the early stages of becoming a derelict attraction to all the urban explorers.
No one gives Clarice a second glance. They either lean against the walls and text on their little cellular phones, smoke cigarettes, or simply stare off into space.
The Malk looks around for somebody who doesn't stand out too much. Her eyes lock onto a man (about her age) who is silently reading a book. He's wearing a flat cap, a faded denim jacket, and a pair of dark jeans.
Clarice casually walks over to the man and stares at him. She looks around and sees that no one's paying them any mind. The Seer gently places a cold, delicate hand on the man's mouth.
She quickly clasps his jaw shut. He doesn't struggle, though he is clearly shocked. He's trying to yell and fight back, but her grip is strong and unwavering.
With preternatural strength, she deftly drags him to a more secluded spot and bites down into his neck. The blood tastes bland and stale, but it's better than absolutely nothing.
As his life's essence leaves him, the Malkavian's (already keen) senses become sharper and more powerful. She only takes what is required. Once her Beast has been appeased, she licks the puncture wounds shut and gently lets the man's body slide onto the ground.
She looks around and is thankful to have chosen a spot away from the crowd. Clarice then checks for a pulse. It's faint, but it's there all the same. The Seer is thankful that she can still sense a heartbeat.
He should recover within the next hour or so, provided another Kindred doesn't come through and decide to bite into him.
Clarice simply gulps, gathers her composure, and walks away with a raised head. She looks at the Prince's thug and taps the roof of his car. He unlocks the doors and the Primogen gets in.
He slyly grins at her, "You made a dead body, didn't ya?"
"Quite the contrary, I assure you."
"Ah, shame. So, where to next, huh? You're not getting rid of me that easily, heh."
Clarice gestures in some vague direction, "There's a cemetery nearby. I would like to go there, please."
The goon's eyebrows shoot up, "A cemetery, huh? Eugh. OK. Do I wanna know why?"
Clarice plainly answers, "My husband is there. I simply want to...talk with him."
The driver frowns and nods, "Gotcha. OK. Just tell me where the place is. And uh...I'm sorry for your loss, lady. But I still gotta tell Prince Antonio about what you've been up to."
The Malk dismissively waves her hand, "Thank you for your condolences. And do feel free to tell that insufferable Ventrue whatever you want. It's not like the fool can do anything else to me, hmm?"
"Haha. I dunno, lady. I'm just the messenger here."
The drive to where Raymond is buried is a long, mostly silent one. There are a few instances of the guy trying to make some small talk, but he's shot down each time.
Clarice merely looks out the window as the city passes her by. Eventually, they arrive at the cemetery. It's a quiet, solemn place. Miss Barton unbuckles her seatbelt and goes over to where her late husband is buried.
The goon follows behind, though he makes sure to keep his (respectful) distance.
Clarice looks over her shoulder and sighs, "Is that really necessary?"
He shrugs, "I got my orders. And they're pretty strict."
The Malk shakes her head, "But keep your voice down. Please. This is a cemetery. Not some rancorous bar or a rowdy strip club."
"Heh, sure."
Raymond Barton's gravestone is covered in a layer of moss and algae. There are a few white roses laying against the gravestone- they look like they were put there quite recently.
Clarice has been visiting his grave a lot over these past few nights. The Seer kneels down and places a hand on the cold, moist stone.
A few tears of blood begin to trickle down her cheeks as she speaks, "My Raymond, I do miss you so much. There's so much I have to say. So much more than even the whole world could handle. My sweet, gentle soul. My love, my light, and my heart."
She looks around before proceeding, "My eternal sunshine. It's such a shame that neither of us had the chance to grow old together, hmm? Ah, how many wonderful days and nights we could have shared..."
A snapping twig causes Clarice to pause. The Prince's lackey is awkwardly looking around, his arms crossed over his chest. Some ruffling of the nearby bushes can be heard.
He glares in the sound's general direction. He narrows his eyes and doesn't spot anything out of the ordinary. Once convinced that it was "just the wind", he shrugs and pulls out a cigarette.
A few moments later, the Malk resumes her shaky speech, "Raymond, I...I do miss you. Not a single night goes by when I don't think of you. And oh, how much I wish you were here. But the fact of the matter is that you aren't."
The big guy lights his cigarette up and carelessly flicks the match away. He blows a cloud of smoke and coughs a few times.
Clarice grits her teeth but keeps her silence.
She looks at the gravestone and continues, "But I know, deep down, that you are still with me, likely watching from afar. I've had to face...several challenges as of late."
The Prince's lackey grunts and points at Clarice, "OK, this is seriously bugging me. Hey, lady, stay here. I'm gonna go and check out what all that damn irritating noise was. If anyone's lurking nearby, I'm gonna kick their asses, and then--"
He continues muttering as he briskly walks off into the other direction, into the woods. Clarice waits for a few moments before leaning closer to the gravestone and softly whispering something.
She whips her head around when she senses an unusual presence lurking nearby. She calls out, "Hello, is anybody there?"
Light footsteps approach. It's a strange man wearing a simple blue suit and a pair of round sunglasses. He's got a relatively carefree expression on his face and is wearing an unusually wide smile.
He stretches out both his hands and calmly announces, "I mean you no harm."
Clarice feels a foreboding chill running up her undead spine. She swallows hard and looks around, her eyes darting to and fro, "Who are you? How did you get in here?"
The man tilts his head and asks, "Are you Clarice Barton, by any chance? The entity that identifies as a...Kindred of a Clan known as...Malkavian?"
The Malk gasps. Her claws pop out, as do her fangs.
The strange man takes a few steps back and holds up his hands, "Now, now, now. There is absolutely no need for any of that. I mean you absolutely no harm whatsoever--"
He stops and squints at the gravestone, "--Miss Barton, yes?"
The Seer hisses at the mysterious man, "Who are you? And how do you--how do you know about me? I don't believe we've met before; and I do have quite a good remembrance of faces."
He lowers his hands, "I was sent here by my master to deliver an important message. One that concerns the current state of affairs, especially pertaining to the future and the fate of another entity within your...Clan."
"Tell me who you are first..."
The strange man bows slightly, "Ah, yes. How impolite of me. My name is Victor Angel. And I serve a master that even a mind like yours cannot begin to comprehend."
Clarice's mouth hangs open, "You--you work for who, exactly?"
Victor's smile grows, "At the moment, I am not fully permitted to divulge such intimate details. Now, if I may, I would like to share a most important message from the one who sent me."
He keeps his clammy hands fully visible as he takes a few cautious steps forward. His movements are slow and fluid. He is clearly trying not to agitate the Malk, though her Beast is beginning to demand that she lunge out and attack him.
Clarice watches him approach her with a wary eye.
Victor's voice is smooth and comforting, "Miss Barton, my master wishes to relay a message to you."
Clarice grits her teeth and demands, "Who is this so-called 'master' of yours, Mr. Angel?"
The Angel of the God-Machine smirks lightly, "I believe that you have been looking for it for quite a short time now, yes? I am told that you have already prodded around and asked of it. But you haven't gone as far in as you would have liked, I'm afraid. Yes or no?"
Clarice's eyes widen, "What are you talking about? Y--Yo--You're certainly not--"
Victor leans in close, "Yes. I am but a humble cog of the God-Machine. And even then, calling myself a 'cog' is being far too self-aggrandizing. However, that is beside the point."
Clarice's lips quiver, "So--so--so, you're here to...what? Silence me for asking questions?"
Victor clicks his tongue, "Oh, come now, Clarice. We are not like your Society. The God-Machine, at best, barely registers you as a threat to its boundless existence and inscrutable machinations."
He gestures to the direction where the Prince's lackey went off in, "Don't worry. He will be gone for the exact amount of time it takes for us to conclude our pleasant little conversation. I'm sure you have certain...queries."
Victor adds, "If I am authorized, I shall do my best to answer them."
Clarice hesitantly nods and begins, "What is this--this God-Machine entity?"
"That's a broad question with no easy answer, I'm afraid. If you keep searching, you shall undoubtedly find...an answer. Whether it shall be satisfactory or not, is something only the future can tell. I cannot provide anything more, at the moment."
The Malkavian Primogen inquires, "What did your master wish to relay to me?"
Victor grins, "Ah, the meat and potatoes of the matter, eh? The divine calculations were, as always, correct. There is a young woman in this city who shows great promise to my master. We would like for you to...meet her, guide her, and..."
After an ominous pause, he whispers, "...prepare her for her ultimate purpose."
Clarice's eyebrows knit together, "Her ultimate purpose? What could this young woman possibly do for an entity as--as vast and all-encompassing as--as--"
Her entire body shakes, she can't even bring herself to speak the entity's name.
Victor holds up a finger, "The entity that I serve is interested in her, because of her unique qualities, her fascinating insights, and her potential. I'm afraid that neither you nor I have the necessary authorization to comprehend the full extent of its motives with this promising young woman."
Clarice glares at the strange man, "Who even is she? I need a name, not all this mumbo-jumbo! Kindly get to the point."
"I'm not authorized to reveal anything more than her basic details, at the moment. Besides, she does not go by her birth name at the moment. I feel strongly that you have an inkling as to who I am referring to."
The Malkavian Primogen ponders for a bit before her face becomes a blank slate. Her expression falls. Her eyes lose focus. The only sound coming from her is a weak, trembling whisper, "Oh...oh...I--I see."
"Yes. You do know, don't you?"
Clarice gulps and looks into Victor's eyes, "What could you possibly want with someone like her?!"
Victor lifts his chin, "I am here to tell you that if you wish to see what lies beyond the veil, then you shall have to stand aside and let us...take her away. She is a special sort of individual, after all."
He pauses, "I am not permitted to share anything else, unfortunately."
Clarice almost raises her voice, "I will NOT let you harm another Malkavian in this Domain! I've already heard of what your God-Machine did to Dallas and I shall NOT sit by idly and watch as--"
Victor chuckles, "My, oh my. What happened with the entity referred to as Dallas was unfortunate, yes. He was not our chosen one. He simply took a peek behind the veil and was unprepared for what he had found, overwhelmed by what his eyes had seen."
Clarice growls, "And how do I know you don't have a similar fate in mind for Ma--her?"
Mr. Angel adjusts his suit sleeves, "You'll have to trust us. We only want what is in the best interests of all creatures, great and small. She will have an important role to play. A role that only she can uniquely fulfill. That is all I am permitted to disclose, Ms. Barton."
He ominously puts in, "Fates far worse await this promising young woman unless we, her chosen protectors, step in and save her from herself...and those who mean her harm."
Clarice lowers her head and mutters, "So, I'm just supposed to sit idly by and--and what? Just hope that she waltzes into your trap...or do you wish for me to bring her to you people?"
Victor equably answers, "You shall have to do nothing of the sort. It has already been determined. The holy algorithms are never wrong, Miss Barton."
He looks her over, "I see that you are a bit conflicted about this matter. But it is all for her own good, of that you can be wholly assured."
Clarice bitterly retorts, "Forgive me if I don't place any confidence in either you or your so-called 'master'."
"Such an attitude is understandable. I am simply the messenger. As for my master, it is best not to even try and fathom its true intentions. Many have tried- people greater than you in all ways- and they have miserably failed."
Clarice glares at the strange man. He's still maintaining a polite, almost cheerful tone.
She murmurs, "I was actually on my way to speak with her tonight..."
He interjects, "Then, do exactly that. The celestial patterns have aligned for the chosen one to ascend. All is proceeding exactly as the grand design of the God-Machine dictates."
Clarice confesses, "I've spent quite a few days, deep in my day-long rest, attempting to find out what this God-Machine really is. I've heard stories of all kinds, rumors that defy basic logic, and incomprehensible legends that simple boggle the mind."
Victor turns his gaze heavenwards and states, "Then it is not time for you to find out. Speaking of time, it is best that I left you to your own devices."
Clarice frowns, "If--if I warn her--"
Victor smoothly cuts her off, "You can warn her all you want, Clarice Barton. It shall happen, it must happen, and it will happen. Her fate has already been determined by powers greater than even your Clan's Antediluvian. Malkav is his name, correct?"
Clarice emptily stares at the strange man, "You--you are not an ordinary mortal, are you? You're something else. Something..."
"Unusual, strange? I am from what they'd call a parallel universe, an alternate timeline, another dimension. The God-Machine gave me a purpose in this iteration of planet Earth, and I have followed that purpose with absolute loyalty. It has, admittedly, led me to many, many wondrous and bizarre places."
He chuckles and goes on, "Do not interfere with our great plans. We would hate to make an enemy out of someone as...nice as you. You can find out about my master in your own time. Perhaps you shall be granted the privilege of learning the truth. Or perhaps you won't. Only time will tell."
Clarice shakes her head, "How--how can I be sure that your...master ensures her safety?"
The Angel shrugs elaborately, "Even I am not wholly privy to such information. I am simply here to ensure that the Infrastructure is in its proper working order."
Clarice crosses her arms, "So, is your master a kind and merciful entity?"
"It is, broadly speaking, a force of neutrality in this timeline. Kind and merciful when needed, but quite the opposite when the situation necessitates a more...forceful hand. It is not a cruel or evil being, and I would not serve it if such were the case."
"Poppycock. Somehow, I get the feeling that you're only serving it because you desperately need something from it."
"Haha. You are quite the sharp Malkavian, aren't you? My master can be harsh, uncompromising, and brutal, yes. I myself have taken part in things I'd rather not speak about, but it's all for a greater purpose. My personal qualms are of little consequence. As are yours."
Victor looks into Clarice's dead eyes and sees the doubts and uncertainty swirling within them.
He calmly reassures her, "You needn't worry about my master or the fate of the chosen young woman. She will be more than she ever was, more than even she could fully comprehend or dream of becoming."
Victor's tone is firm, yet reassuring, "Going forward, she will surely have a crucial role to play in the grand design of this wonderful world of...yours, dare I say...ours. So, please, let us have her. Stay out of our way."
He wistfully says, "Even after all these decades, my feelings about this version of planet Earth are...mixed. Though, I'd say that overall, I enjoy it. There is still so much of it left to see and experience. But I do miss my...world just as much."
Victor checks his wristwatch and announces, "Now, sadly, our conversation comes to an end. I can hear that man's plodding footsteps. Do as you must and please, do not get in our way. We can safely, peacefully co-exist."
Clarice glares at the odd-looking man, "What is this 'grand design' of yours?"
Victor turns on heel, twirls his fingers, and silently strides away, into the darkness. The shadows swallow him up and his footsteps calmly fade away.
A few moments later, the Prince's henchman reappears, looking thoroughly irritated. He mutters a few curses and dusts some leaves off his clothing.
"Damn trees, damn bushes, and damn everything. This is why I don't do the whole nature thing! Grrr!"
He stops in his tracks and can't believe that Clarice is still there. He walks up to her and incredulously asks, "Uh, hey, are you OK? Did anything happen while I was gone?"
"No."
"Honestly, lady? I'm actually surprised that you're still here! I thought you'd have bailed--"
The Malk cuts him off with a raised hand, "Don't get ahead of yourself. Come, now. I'll tell you where I wish to go next."
The big man groans and follows behind, "Fine. But just so you know, I'm tired as hell...and hungry. For actual food, not someone's warm, sweaty-ass neck."
The Prince's lackey gets in the car and Clarice follows shortly. The goon sighs heavily and lights another cigarette.
"You people don't breathe, right? So, I'm gonna smoke this shit all night long, just like the damn doctor ordered me NOT to do. Ahem, anyway, where are we going now, lady?"
Miss Barton stares straight ahead and blandly answers, "The Prairie Ridge."
The man whistles as some smoke comes out his nose, "Whoa, lady. Looking to buy a fancy new place, huh?"
Clarice lets out a long, tired sigh and turns her head aside. The fella gets her message loud and clear. The car's engine roars back to life, and the vehicle speeds off.
"Yeah, yeah, yeah. Keep your silence."
Clarice does exactly that. She's so deep in silence that when they do arrive at the intended destination, she is snapped back to reality when the lackey snaps his fingers in front of her face.
"Yo, we're here! The Ridge, right?"
The high-class apartment complex, as always, exudes a sense of sophistication and class. Clarice wordlessly exits the car and tells her "chauffeur" to make himself scarce.
"Well, shit, I'd much rather get some sleep and food in my stomach, lady, but whatever. And no, you're not gettin' rid of me that easily."
Clarice glowers at him and commands, "Don't you dare follow me inside."
The man harshly chuckles, "Lady, I'm not even sure I can afford to breathe the air in that fancy place, OK?"
"Hmph."
"Yeah, yeah. Hey, you know any good places to grab an...affordable bite around here? I'm talking grub, not gourmet."
Clarice shrugs, "Look around or try asking...but do it nicely."
The lackey grins and gives the vampire a thumbs-up, "OK, Sesame Street. I'll be sure to say please and thank you, too."
The Malk rolls her eyes, "You should always be doing that. And you're going to report everything to Antonio, right?"
The big guy laughs, "Hell yeah, I gotta. It's what he's fuckin' paying me for! And I badly need the cash before I get the hell outta dodge and leave this crazy city. Oh, and he also said that I'd get a nice fat bonus if I kept an eye on you, too."
His expression softens a bit, "I need the cash, lady. Please, don't make me hurt you. I know enough to know that I wouldn't walk away from a fight with someone...like you."
