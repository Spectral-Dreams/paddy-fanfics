Chapter 23: Killer Of Killers
It's about 4:30 AM. The morning sun is peeking through the buildings of this vast, crushing, Midwestern metropolis. It's not long before the Kindred in this time zone succumb to the pull of the day-sleep.
Once that great ball of fire fully emerges, the Kindred are fully "asleep", and their Unliving bodies are now undergoing a process of rest and rejuvenation.
At a local coffee shop, a middle-aged man with a large gut is sipping his steaming hot coffee. He wolfs down his 6th piece of French Toast, dusts his hands, wipes his mouth, and steps out onto the cracked pavement.
It's brutally hot outside. Luckily, this man's wife reminded him to wear his sunscreen before leaving for work. He gets inside his aged garbage truck that hasn't been upgraded since 1999, starts it up, and makes his way through the winding city streets.
Despite doing something necessary for the continual function of civilization, this garbage truck driver is woefully underpaid. He looks at the brochures for colleges that his older son had handed him on his way out the door.
They cost a lot of money. A lot. Money which this man simply does not have. There have been whispers of a strike amongst the man's co-workers, but it's just hush-hush for now.
Then again, the prospect of going on a strike would be welcome amongst the rank-and-file. It's only a matter of time until it happens.
The sun continues to beat down on the city below. Cars chug along wide roads. Buildings of glass and steel stand tall in the bright sky. People shuffle about, heading from 1 place to another.
They all seem to have a purpose and a destination to go to. Well, most of them, anyway.
Mr. Strickland's people have been readying themselves. They're still on the prowl, on the lookout for the "monster" who killed 3 of their fellow hitters.
A "monster" whose true nature remains a mystery to these jumped-up thugs. All they know is that Mr. Strickland has generously offered $300,000 to whomever (solo or in squad) can "neutralize" Markham Reed and bring his cold corpse to him.
Additionally, Mr. Strickland himself is personally overseeing certain aspects of this "operation". He's busy coordinating his little "family" of bloodthirsty maniacs, ordering them around, going through the intel reports they compile, and so on.
In a way, Mr. Strickland's men and women are nothing more than a bunch of bloodthirsty simpletons who can take instructions like golden retrievers.
While the sun sets, these mouth-breathing psychos scour the city for a man resembling Markham Reed. Rocks are turned over, informants are asked (nicely), and all sorts of other shady things take place.
But none of these efforts bear fruit. So far.
Once the sun has fully set, the Kindred in this part of the globe begin to stir from the cold embrace of their day-sleep.
Markham twitches on the hard mattress. He's pulled into a dreamy space where the skies above are dark as coal. There are no stars, no moon, no clouds, nothing. Nothing except for the vast and empty sky above.
Diana shimmers into view. Her eyes are extremely baggy, and her hair is all over the place. The dream version of the Baali is sitting on a nearby lawn chair.
There is a red shooting star roaring right beside him.
Diana's lips are blue and she's shaking all over, "Why--why didn't you save me? Why didn't you HELP ME?!"
Markham springs up from his seat and tries to reach forward, "I tried! I'm sorry! I'm so sorry!"
The long-gone woman shakes her head and glares at him, "You can't even help yourself, let alone someone else!"
Daniella pops up from the ground, covered in a black goopy tar of some kind. She wipes some of it off her face, "What about me, little brother? How come you didn't save me?"
Markham takes a step back and gasps in shock. Words do not seem to be able to form in his mouth. He opens and closes his mouth several times.
The tar-covered Daniella tilts her head in an eerie way, "You should have done more THAT NIGHT!"
Markham screams out, "I tried! I'm so sorry! Daniella, Diana...I'm so--so--sorry that I didn't do more to help you."
He takes a few deep breaths and runs his fingers through his hair, "I don't know how you 2 are here, or why you're even here..."
Rhonda materializes out of thin air. She's dressed in her waitress uniform, her hair done up in her usual "victory rolls" style, and she's wearing her name tag.
She glares at Markham, "How do I know that I'm safe with somebody like ya, huh? How do I know that you won't kill me or worse, huh?"
Markham is utterly speechless. He opens and closes his mouth a few times, but no words are coming out.
Adding to the pressure, Jamie Pollux can be seen levitating in the distance. She gracefully lands a few feet away from the vampire, a totally neutral look on her face.
She looks at her co-worker and flatly asks, "Do you ever plan on telling me...or are you just gonna lug your big secret around with you till the end of time itself, huh?"
Markham sees a rundown log cabin the distance. He runs to it with all his might. However, the faster he runs, the longer the distance seems to be between him and that cabin.
A shadowy whisper caresses Mr. Reed's ears. It feels like a feather duster being rubbed against his ears, "Come to me. Find me, find me. Compact me. Why do you avoid me, Childe of the Infernal?"
Markham is panting, doing all he can to run to that goddamn log cabin. He does not seem to be getting any closer to it. His body is shaking, and he's sweating blood all over.
A massive shadow appears from behind the fern trees. The leaves blacken and begin to crumble into ashes. An enormous shadowy being with horns emerges from the falling trees.
It's a humanoid, completely devoid of any distinguishing features. It's merely a pitch-black silhouette that's quite tall, gangly, and vaguely human in presentation.
It stands right beside a lake of blood. Its black body blocks the orange-red sun from view.
Markham's eyes widen. He gulps and falls to his knees as the figure stretches out its hand and envelops him in its shadowy tendrils that pop out from its hand.
It whispers into the Baali Neonate's ears, "The time has long since passed. The Beyond will give you your strength. Blood is the price for greater power, the powers of...the Beyond. Compact with me--"
Markham's eyes widen even more and he begins to scream, "AHHHHHHHH!" his skin feels like it's boiling. Blood incessantly pours out from every orifice in his body.
Back in the real world, Markham awakens with a wheezing gasp.
The last lingering whispers echo in his mind, "We shall meet very soon...very soon. I can help you..."
Markham frantically shakes his head and grunts. He feels like there's something sitting on his chest and that it's getting heavier by the moment.
He forces a few breaths through his dead lungs and takes great effort to get out of the hard mattress.
"Ugrrrh."
The Baali stands up looks around what has been his "room" for the past few weeks. He rubs his pallid face a few times and sluggishly makes his way to the bathroom for a quick shower.
His gray eyes are heavy and baggy, and his hair is unkempt and messy. His solid-colored t-shirt looks like it has seen better nights, and there's a slight tear in his shorts.
"Damn!"
After showering, Markham wraps the towel around his waist and picks his outfit for the night. He decides on something casual, but not too much so- pleated black pants, an untucked, long-sleeved, button-down yellow shirt, and some plain brown shoes.
He runs a comb through his chin length hair and looks at himself in the mirror. He bears his fangs and stares at his reflection in the mirror.
"I'm not sure how much longer I can take this."
For the time being, Mr. Reed's investigation is on hold. Additionally, he has no new leads to follow and has hit a bit of a brick wall with this case (in more ways that 1!).
Therefore, Markham decides to "lay low" for a while. But a workaholic like him, devoted to the job of investigating financial malfeasance, would be very unlikely to do such a thing.
He decides to go over what he DOES have, what he does know so far. So, he flops down on the couch and turns on his laptop.
After a few minutes, Diego emerges with Marisol in tow. Marisol is wearing a red-and-black dress with matching heels and earrings while her boyfriend is dressed in a polo shirt and corduroy pants.
The 2 Gangrel look like they're going somewhere nice tonight. Markham looks up at them, eyebrow raised.
Diego rubs his palms together and anticipates the Baali's query by answering, "Ah, yeah. Marisol's taking off work tonight. I thought I'd uh...go around town with her, drive around, see if there's any new places worth checking out."
Marisol scoffs, "Pfft. What new places? It's Flimont City. It's only greasy diners and liquor stores, and a bunch of people who just wanna be left alone."
Markham shrugs, "Well, enjoy yourselves. I'll be working until you both decide on where you want to go."
Diego and Marisol exchange glances. The former asks, "You still sticking your nose in your work, ese? I thought- after all the shit that's happened- that you'd be giving that a break, y'know...chilling, relaxing, thinking about--"
Marisol cuts in, an intense look on her face, "And don't you get my man into trouble like that ever again, OK? As it is, we are already having you as our guest in our Haven."
Markham politely replies, "Y'know, I--I can always find myself a hotel--"
Diego glares at his girlfriend before holding up both of his hands, "Ah, ah, ah, ah, ah, ah. We've already been over this, homes. You're staying with us- in our Haven- until you're done with whatever the hell it is you came here to do."
Marisol sighs in frustration, "How can he still work and do his investigation when the subjects of said investigation are trying to have him killed? If it was me, I would--"
The Baali interjects, "Look, I understand your frustration. Believe me, I do. But there's nothing much that I can do at the moment. I just have to lay low and hope that these assassins don't come back for me. That's it."
Marisol crosses her arms and grumbles, "Fine. As long as they don't turn my--our Haven into a shooting gallery...D?"
Diego gently pats her back and looks at his guest, "Markham, look...we appreciate all that you've done for us. Y'know, you didn't have to pay Raul for my pickup's repairs--"
Markham shakes his head and responds, "Diego. Look. It was nothing, alright? After all you've done for me, the kindness you've both shown me, it was the least I could do, alright?"
He adds, "It's--it's the least I can do." There is genuine sincerity in his voice.
Marisol's expression softens a bit, "We are truly grateful for your gesture of kindness."
Diego nods contemplatively, "Yeah. Absolutely. What--what she said. Thank you. Really, I mean it, man."
The Baali warmly smiles back at the 2 Ferals before turning his full attention back to his laptop.
Marisol is taken by Diego into a corner by Diego, out of earshot. The latter turns to his lady and flatly states, "Markham has been through a lot over these past nights."
"I know that, D. You think my work has been any easier? Why do you think I asked for some time off, hmm? You think I did it for fun? I know we need the money, but...I need a break."
Diego presses his lips together and gently rubs her shoulders, "I know, chica. I know. Once Markham's done and out of here, I'm gonna seriously look into getting myself a proper job with proper hours...and a proper uniform, heh. I heard there's some vacancies at ShopSave."
He lowers his head a little, "Just low-level stuff, but I ain't gonna complain."
Marisol sniffs, "That's kind of you, D. Thank you. I know that we've only been together for, what...4 years, but I really appreciate all the sacrifices you've made."
Diego's eyes widen, "Sacrifices? Marisol, it's YOU who's been making all the sacrifices! And for that, I will always--I love you so much. I don't know where I'd be if you hadn't come into my Unlife, mi corazon."
The 2 Outlanders share a brief, but passionate kiss, their tongues swirling around inside each other's open mouths.
They break the kiss and stare dreamily, hungrily into each other's eyes.
Diego cups his girlfriend's cheeks and firmly reassures her, "Look, I know things are pretty tough right now, but we'll get through it. Remember...we're Gangrel, we're survivors. We've made it this far, haven't we?"
Marisol's smile is weak, "We...we are, but are you sure about us surviving this? Things don't look so good out there, D...the news, the jobs situation, this year's election, it's all a mess."
Diego kisses his lady on the cheek, "We'll make it. We'll do our best to make it. That much I--I promise you."
A lone tear of blood flows down his cheek. Marisol gently wipes it away and licks it. She then smiles at her man.
"D...let's take care of what we have to do tonight, and then...let's go somewhere...quiet, somewhere private."
There's a knowing look in her eyes. Her tone is husky, her words slightly suggestive. She gives him a little wink before walking over to the kitchen.
Diego chuckles, bites his lip, and is already anticipating the fun that's to come later on. He's already fantasizing about what's gonna go down, because when these 2 Gangrel get started...they're gonna be at it for a while.
And there is quite a lot of grunting involved, too.
Anyway, Markham spends the next hour or so going over what he already does know about Luciano and Paul. He has enough to build a half-decent case against those 2, but he knows that it's simply insufficient for a successful prosecution.
He wants to go after both of them, but he knows that they are both connected to a lot of powerful people in the city, from the business crowd to the local government to the financial institutions themselves.
Not to mention, Luciano Donovan Senior who is a behemoth of a man. All he'd have to do is shoot a phone call to somebody very important, and the entire case would be tossed out faster than one can blink their eyes.
So, Markham needs to be careful about this. He's not about to give up just yet, but he's not about to rush in blindly without a plan.
He rubs the side of his head and groans, "If only I knew HOW they're doing it, then--then I'd be able to make the proper plans to get at them..."
He sighs in frustration and growls, "I need a whole lot more information on their operation. These returns...goddammit. Hmph."
Perhaps finding the "how" was right around the corner, so to speak.
The night progresses at an even pace. Marisol and Diego left about 3 hours ago. Either way, the night's still young. Markham is lazily splayed on the couch.
His eyes are half-open, and "Rush Hour" (1998) is playing on the small TV. The Baali is barely paying attention to the movie, his mind wandering hither and yon.
He thinks about that weird dream that he had before being yanked awake. He can't get that dream out of his head, no matter how hard he tries.
The Demon thinks out loud, "What...was it? Was it actually them or my--my own impression of all the people in my Unlife? Hmm, either way, I gotta do my best to NOT get 'em involved, for their sakes."
He closes his eyes and breathes in deeply. He slowly exhales and begins to think about what he's going to do next.
Markham hits the mute button on the remote and paces around the Haven for a few moments. He stretches himself out and groans softly.
His Hunger nags him and is yet to be sated. However, the Demon is too preoccupied with the investigation (and what to do next) to feed, despite the gnawing sensation coursing throughout his entire body.
Mr. Reed grunts, "Rrrrrrgh. Fine. I'll see who I can find nearby..."
Before leaving, Markham leaves a note for his hosts in case they return before he does. He steps out and closes the door behind him.
The Demon looks down both ends of the street and decides to walk towards the direction that leads to the less-busy parts of this particular neighborhood.
The moon's pale light shines over the streets of Flimont City. A few stars twinkle in the skies above. Markham walks away from Diego and Marisol's Haven, pockets shoved deep in his pant pockets.
He blends into the shadows and takes a look at his surroundings. It's a fairly mundane suburban neighborhood. The pavement is showing its age, the trees above are solid and healthy, and there are some parked cars in the driveways of various homes.
The Baali sniffs the air around him. It smells stale, musty, and dry. There aren't many people around, either. Some warm breeze blows through the city.
Markham continues to walk down the dark road, his shoes softly thumping on the pavement below.
He sniffs the air again and grimaces when he picks up a foul scent. A person approaches from his right. Mr. Reed slows his pace as he takes a look at the woman in question.
She's wearing a leather jacket and tight pants. Her features are obscured by the shadows all around, and she's looking down at her feet.
Markham thinks nothing of it and walks on, shrugging indifferently.
"Don't get jumpy now."
While walking on, he does steal a glance over his shoulder every now and then. He can still hear that woman's footsteps. His eyes narrow and his nostrils flare.
He can feel another beating heart nearby. So, he picks up the pace and breaks out into a sprint. Markham turns left at the intersection and sees that there is a small playground with some benches scattered around.
There's nobody else here, thankfully.
The woman in the leather jacket quickly dials a number, peering through the bushes to watch the Baali as he paces a tight circle.
A familiar voice answers on the other end, "Ms. Purple. Status report?"
Uh-oh.
The mortal assassin has a devious smirk on her face. She watches Markham walk back and forth, like a caged tiger pacing in their pen, waiting to be shot at or put down.

They are more than happy to join the hunt for Mr. Reed...and split the fee, of course.

They're closing in.
