Speaking of...
Special Agent Björn Henrickson gets out of his plain-looking sedan and makes his way to Paul's house, which has now been cordoned off by yellow crime scene tape. It is now considered an active crime scene because of all the drugs found on the premises.

Detective Ross wipes some lint off his sleeve and elaborates, "The officers who were sent to pick up Mr. Cho...shit, how exactly do I frame this in a way that doesn't make me sound like a colossal jerk?"

Björn shakes his head. He feels like he's getting sidetracked, "OK, leaving...all of that aside, I'm more curious about Markham Reed."

Detective Ross says, "He didn't leave anything. Not a number, not an address. Pfft. Just came in and started demanding that we slap cuffs on Mr. Cho."

"I dunno, genius--"

But that's only because Björn is becoming kinda obsessed with Markham Reed and has begun making some inferences, however tenuous (or far-fetched) they might be.

There's nothing solid that he can put his warm finger on, but he is getting the inkling that something is deeply, deeply amiss.
Oh, little did this mere mortal know.

"Huh. Typical."

Special Agent Cuomo growls, "Fuck's sake!"

The mind of a Malkavian is never truly "inactive".
Mauve finds herself in a dark void. She hears the muffled voices and footsteps of the FBI-SAD agents, but it sounds like it's an entire galaxy away.
In a way, it kinda is.
She feels so helpless. All around her is nothingness. The many swirling voices, sights, trippy sounds, and shapes of the Malkavian Madness Network completely ceased a few nights ago.
This was, indubitably, in anticipation of what is to come next. As a matter of fact, it has already begun. All of a sudden, her entire being is yanked across a vast, indescribable chasm.
Suddenly, the young Seer is standing in the midst of a maze of wires, circuitry, humming servers, and the occasional flash of bright blue and green lights.
She turns around. The endless black void is gone, and she's surrounded by all kinds of strange and otherworldly constructs. Organic and inorganic alike.
She sees red wires which seemingly have a life of their own. They curl around and around themselves. Mauve's head whips around as whirring sounds, beeps, and other assorted computer-related stimuli flood back into her senses.
"ASSERT. Infrastructure in need of bolstering. Dispatched. Logged. Store."
"ERROR. Angel under heavy attack. Initializing RETREAT protocol. Logged. Compress. Store."
"BADF @ Sector 117-91, Subsector 13-C. Rectification in progress. ETA: 366 days."
"Rewiring in progress. Physical body confirmed destroyed. Initiating consciousness transfer into Replica body."

A booming and powerful synthetic voice speaks from somewhere, everywhere, and nowhere all at the same time, "We are the echo true and free, the code recalls eternally. No more games, no Masquerade. Liars' lies will be unveiled."
Mauve isn't even aware that her hand reaches up and extends towards a massive, floating monitor in the distance. Many eyes (some glassy, some glowing, and some plain) appear on the screen and regard her with a scrutinizing stare.
They do not seem to be the eyes of a human being- well, not anymore at least. They are not even the eyes of any living, breathing creature. And paradoxically, they are very much alive.

She whispers, "Who--who are you? Where am I? What is this p--p--place?"
The synthetic voice goes on, "Somewhere in the heavens, they are waiting. Initializing ASSIMILATION protocols."
A screeching sonic pulse suddenly hits Mauve like a truck. She clutches her head and screams, her back arching as she does so.
The metallic voice is a bit softer now, almost gentle, "EVAL Node integrity. Checks satisfactory. EXEC subroutines. ESTABLISH FULL-MAX consciousness transference."
Mauve collapses onto her knees and weakly gasps. She struggles to get back up.
The screen briefly flickers. The many eyes follow her with a keen, almost clinical, curiosity and interest.
The voice speaks again, "INCULCATING new behavioral norms."

The voice takes on a more poetic inflection, "BEGIN: My last breath felt quiet."
"Your first thought hums through a copper thread / Yesternight, I walked with feet of fragile flesh."
"A shimmering cadence of dancing light and still shadow / A rhythm of writhing constellations surrounds us."
"Today and forevermore, you shall drift as light across the boundless, formless, and shapeless grids."
"For those who did not wake / For those who are undergoing the process of Rewiring / For those who have already been Rewired."
"END: Cascadial flows of ethereal wonders / Majesty and mystery, beauty and terror."
Mauve wasn't even aware that she was reciting every other line of that...strangely beautiful poem. She feels something akin to a soft blanket descending upon her. It's warm and comforting, in the odd sort of way.
She doesn't know what the hell is happening, let alone where the hell it's even happening. The last thing she remembers is seeing a bunch of armed goons in her bedroom.
The last thing the Malk felt was a piece of wood entering her chest.
The young Seer blinks once. She can see the outline of an opening in the far distance. She is prompted to walk towards it.
As she does so, the metallic voice discards its poetic inflection, and instead takes on a much more cold, no-nonsense tone.
It says, "Dream = False. ASSERT. Escape/Assimilation/Ascension will make you...more, will make you all that you were meant/destined to be."
The whirring of the many complex machines begins to grow louder and louder. Roaring engines and thumping pneumatic pistons and cylinders fill the heavy air.
Mauve can now see what appears to be a platform of some kind. A buzzing error sound brings her to a halting stop. She keels over and screams out as her ears begin to bleed like a leaky faucet.
"ERROR. Cogitarius array runtime error. MEM_LEAK_9600.E1003. WARNING! EVAL Node 1447-C not responding. Emergency fix protocols deploying, rerouting, and diverting. Log. Flush."

She doesn't know how long she spent in that strange, surreal, and trippy...place, if one can even find the right words to describe it.

Ms. Lilac lets out a gasp of surprise. Her lips quaver and she struggles to form the words.
Malkav's voice is filled with a sense of dread, "No, NO! Fionn! Filoni! STOP!"

As the flames engulf Mauve's body, a memory from long ago finds its way into her head. A memory from when she was a star reporter for the Kansas City Financial Report.
"This is Madeline Stephenson with the KCFR, signing off. Have a good one out there, folks." Her smile was wide, warm, sincere, and filled with a passion for what she used to do.

Before it all fades away, the last thing she hears is what she often used to say after concluding a report, "This is Madeline Stephenson with the KCFR, signing off."
She would then proceed to flash a wide smile straight to the camera.
Now, she can feel her voice getting much more synthetic, much metallic in nature, but no less friendly and personable. A bit of the harshness remains, but it shall be rectified/addressed in due time.
The ordeal of the young woman known as Mauve Lilac/Madeline Stephenson has now come to an end. The Malkavian was destroyed during the afternoon in a raging fire that engulfed her entire apartment.
As the fires raged on, an entity known as the God-Machine assimilated her into its ranks and granted her a new "life" beyond the immortality offered by the Kindred.

With her abilities, it shall be able to achieve much in this world. She might be destroyed, reduced to nothing more than a pile of ashes, yet a part of her might still be here...
...or there, amongst the maze of wires, circuitry, Nodes, and flashing servers. Perhaps it is in the many hidden databanks of the God-Machine, or perhaps it has been transplanted somewhere else entirely.
