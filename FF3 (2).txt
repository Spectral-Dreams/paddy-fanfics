Chapter 21: Vendetta
Sunrise is a particularly monotonous affair. It emerges and bathes the city in its harsher (than usual) yellow light. It's glowing outside today.
Soon enough, all the Kindred in this part of the world are dragged into the comforting, cold embrace of the day-sleep. Even Markham, despite all that had happened, was pulled in quite easily.
In all fairness, what he did was self-defense and Mr. Pink, on account of being a war veteran, was an abject piece of trash who shan't be missed by any good, decent, and honest folks.
And neither should the rest of the scum working under the command of Mr. Strickland.
That dark, foreboding thing (which isn't the Beast) continues to stir within Mr. Reed's Unliving body. A Daimonion from the Beyond has focused its full attention on Markham and wishes to...compact with him.
That's the Baali Bloodline's Compulsion- their infernal Blood compels them to enter into some sort of a deal with (allegedly) diabolical forces from beyond the Tellurian in exchange for greater powers and such.
Ain't it lovely?
Be that as it may, Markham's not gonna get the answers from within and the few Kindred that he DOES know in this city won't be able to help him out in this regard, either.
No. He's gonna have to get in touch with somebody from his Bloodline. Lucky for him, there's a Baali Coven right under Beaumont Boulevard.
The question is- what happens when Mr. Reed decides to get in touch with his fellow Demon Worshippers? Is this the only way forward?
Let's face it: his investigation is fucked. The bozos that he's looking into have tried, 3 times, to have him bumped off. They have also failed 3 times, in doing so.
And now with Mr. Strickland personally getting involved, Markham has been marked. Oh, joy.
For now, put the devilry aside and permit the tired vampire to rest. After all the shit that he's been through, he needs it.
The Flimont City International Airport is starting to show its age in certain parts. Certain walls are water-damaged, and the floor is in serious need of a good, deep cleaning.
It's a large, aged structure of glass and steel. Planes whoosh off to destinations far and wide. The duty-free store's a bit pricey. There's plenty of restaurants, ATMs, and gift shops around the terminal areas, however.
There was a drive to originally call this place the Ronald Reagan Freedom Airport Of Flimont City, but thankfully nobody was having it. So, it's just the FCIA now (IATA Code: FIA).
Airport workers, baggy-eyed and tried, go about their duties. They push and pull carts around the place and keep tabs on the respective flights. Many passengers walk by and ask them all sortsa questions.
It ranges from where's the bathroom to queries about any flight delays or whatnot. The staff around here are as friendly and polite as can be, but the stress is getting to 'em, especially the older, veteran workers who've been here for literal decades.
Some are kinda snappy.
2 men from Lancaster-SYS look at their respective watches. They're both dressed in sharp 2-piece business suits, ties, polished shoes, and all the accoutrements that the upper-crust like to adorn themselves with.
A man with a receding hairline takes a deep breath, "Where is this guy? This IS the right pickup spot, right?"
The other man is a few feet shorter and is a bit twitchy, "Yeah, Matt. It's the right fucking pickup spot, alright? You think I'm stupid, you think I was born yesterday?"
Matt glares at the short man and grimaces, "Jesus, Alec. What's gotten into you, huh? What's with the sudden outburst, huh? I mean, seriously?"
The taller man spreads out his arms in disbelief, "All I did was ask you a simple fucking question, dude."
The twitchy one called Alec gives Matt a nasty look before returning his focus to his silver Fossil watch, "It's all this restructuring bullshit. Ever since Laurence goddamn Lancaster bailed out on us, it feels like the whole damn company is in freefall."
"Yeah, no shit. Interim CEO was an unexpected pick, but the board knows what it's doing, so..."
The shorter man laughs bitterly, "You sure about that? You sure that we gonna get our Christmas bonuses this year or...?"
Matt shrugs, "Well, it's hard to say. All the recent negative press has been killin' us in the stock market, valuation of the company's taken a tumble..." his words drift off and die off into silence.
Alec groans and scratches his forehead with his thumb, "Yeah, doom and gloom. I guess I'm gonna have to cancel my ski vacation this year. The mistress was really looking forward to it, heh."
Matt shakes his head and chuckles, "If your freakin' wife finds out..."
Alec dismissively waves his hand and confidently smirks, "Relax, she won't. So, uh...what was this guy's name again? The one that we're supposed to be meeting, picking up, and bringing back?"
Matt rolls his eyes and groans, "Victor Angel."
"Wow. Sounds...odd. Description?"
"Jerome said, and I quote: 'You'll know him when you see him because of how he sticks out in a crowd.' End of quote."
"Hehehehehe, nice."
It isn't long before a throng of people swarm out of the terminal. They come in all ages, genders, shapes, sizes, colors, and nationalities. Most of them look happy and relieved.
Must've been a really cramped-up flight.
The 2 guys from Lancaster-SYS crane their necks and try to find Victor, but they're having some real trouble picking him out from the crowd.
Eventually, the sea of people dissipates all over the place, and they are still left empty-handed.
Alec impatiently taps his foot on the ground and looks at his watch, "C'mon, c'mon, Angel. C'mon..."
Matt adjusts his collar and practices his professional smile a few times, "Just give him a few more minutes. He'll show. The whole board wouldn't have sent us here just for some sorta fluke."
"Uhhhh-huh," Alec is unconvinced, and it shows in his tone, "This dude better show, or else. Seriously, I can't believe that he managed to get a meeting with the entire board."
Matt glares at Alec and dryly states, "He's an important client. Probably our most important one in fuckin' years, man!"
Alec gulps and asks, "What was the project again?"
"Some server and technological infrastructure project. Something like that. Top-secret shit."
"Sounds fucking shady to me."
"I don't care, and neither of us are paid to give a shit. We're just lackeys here to pick up some VIP and ensure that he gets his sit-down with the board. Nothing more, nothing less."
Alec smirks and muses, "A dream fucking job! Getting paid to sit on our asses, getting free flights, free hotels, free cars, blow, hookers, the works, all just to bring some shady guy over to the big building."
Matt groans softly and murmurs, "Don't knock it."
"You got the latest memo?"
"Um...no?"
Alec beams, "Extended holidays...starting tomorrow. Whoo-wee!" he pumps his fist in the air a few times and cackles loudly.
Matt perches an eyebrow skeptically, "What? Seriously? Huh, crazy. Maybe I can finally take my kid fishing."
"Uh-huh."
After a good 12 minutes, a strange-looking man emerges from the crowd of people and walks straight towards Matt and Alec.
He is of medium height and average weight. He has a receding hairline, clammy skin, and somehow looks both 50 and 20 at the same time (not many can pull that off).
His eyebrows are uneven and his cheekbones are pretty high. His nose is unusually long, his eyes are large and very, very dark, almost like voids.
The man stretches out his left hand and smiles widely, showing teeth that are perfectly straight, "Good morning, gentlemen!"
He looks kinda creepy to these 2 guys, and his voice is unusually chipper. There's an edge of nervousness to it, like he's a 1950s radio announcer reading off a script or something.
Alec nervously shakes the outstretched hand and whispers, "Um...are you Victor Angel?"
Mr. Angel shakes Alec's hand tightly and nods, "Oh, yes, indeed. I am he. In the flesh."
Matt furrows his brows, "Do you have any baggage, sir?"
Victor answers with a smile, "Oh, worry not about it; I travel very light and I've already had my possessions brought elsewhere. Now, now, gentlemen, we best not linger about. The board is expecting me, and I do not wish to keep them waiting for a second longer."
Mr. Angel squeezes past the 2 men and walks forward, chin raised high in the air.
Alec mumbles, "Well, he's fuckin' weird."
Matt glowers at his co-worker, "Shut up and get a move on!"
Alec raises his hands, palms facing forward, and shrugs, "Just saying!"
Victor's walking speed is quite fast and the 2 struggle to keep up. His stride is oddly steady, and his gait is confident and purposeful. He puts on a pair of dark sunglasses and looks up at the sun shining down on his face.
It's a fairly long and boring walk to the company limo. Matt and Alec keep exchanging nervous looks, as they're unsure what to say, and Victor keeps going on and taking the sights in.
He's muttering something incoherent and barely audible to himself. He makes these odd hand gestures, like he's measuring and calculating the air around him.
A few of the locals give Victor a strange look as they're walking by. He pays them no heed and continues to murmur to himself.
Alec nervously clears his throat, "Um...sir?"
Victor's smile is soft and eerie at the same time, "Yes?"
"You OK?"
Matt nudges Alec and shoots him a dirty look.
Victor's still smiling as he assures, "All is well, I can assure you of this. All is as it should be, gentlemen. Falling and rising. Rising and falling. It is all a grand part of this wonderful thing that we call life, isn't it?"
Matt's jaw hangs open, "Um..."
Alec nervously nods, "Yeah, yeah, I guess so."
The company's limo is jet-black, sleek, and spacious enough for a mini party (with hookers and blow) to go on inside of it.
Victor opens up the door to the vehicle and gets inside, humming softly to himself and still making those odd gestures and odder murmurs.
Matt overhears something pertaining to "Infrastructure" (!) while Alec swears that he's seen those hand gestures in some random documentary about ancient occult practices.
They both dismiss this and decide to forget about it.
Victor remains cheery and watches the city during the day. He does an encompassing gesture, "My, my, my. The traffic here is...something else. I hope that we won't be late."
Matt blandly points out, "It's Flimont City, Mr. Angel. Traffic is a part and parcel of the daily routine around here, sadly. I hope you like waiting."
Alec chimes in, "Yeah, what he said. I got myself a new SUV because my old beat-up sedan wasn't able to survive the commutes."
Victor tilts his head, "Hmm? And how long is a typical daily commute around here?"
Alec shrugs and trills his lips, "It depends. But it ain't usually measured in minutes."
Victor smirks, "It can be optimized, then. Yes. This...this is something to look into."
Matt side-eyes the strange man, "Um...what?"
Mr. Angel softly chuckles and tugs on the lapels of his blue suit jacket. He dusts off some lint on his matching pant and hums, "All will be revealed in due time. Oh, indeed it will be, yes indeed."
Totally NOT ominous at all.
The drive to the Lancaster-SYS building is tense and long. On the way, Victor asks for the A/C to be turned off so he can get a bit of sunlight.
The sun shines down on Mr. Angel and warms his skin up a little. His skin glistens, and the 2 men with him keep giving him uncomfortable looks.
Matt and Alec sweat and undo the top buttons of their shirts while Victor appears to be unaffected. He just stares up at the sky with those unreadable, void-like eyes of his.
Victor looks at the sweaty duo and asks, "I hope that my delay at the airport wasn't too much of a hassle?"
Matt quickly replies, "No, no, no, of course not."
Alec chimes in, "We're always here for the client. Especially someone as important as yourself."
Victor comments, "It was the airport security. I had to go through those scanners 3 times, I kid you not! 3 times, imagine that! And in all the 3 times I passed through, the machine detected nothing unusual."
The strange man smiles softly, "My ticket was scanned and everything checked out."
Matt muses, "Yeah, well, after what...happened, you can't be too certain these days."
Victor pensively nods, "Yes, a real tragedy indeed."
Alec eyes Victor and tells him, "You're gonna have to go through the same procedure at the main building. It's part of the security protocol that we have in place."
Mr. Angel laughs heartily and leans forward to give the man an approving pat on the shoulder, "That's very good to know!"
Matt inquires, "It's not every day that a client convinces the entire board to have a sit-down with 'em. So, what's up?"
Victor shrugs and makes that odd gesture with his hand again, "Yes, yes, well, sometimes it takes some doing. With the utmost respect, it really is not your concern, my good sirs."
He claps his hands together and warns them, "Stay as far away from it as you possibly can. Enjoy your holidays, which I believe are starting from...tomorrow, yes?"
Alec gasps lightly. How did this very important client know that? Matt shakes his head and shrugs, not wishing to think about it too deeply.
Mr. Angel smiles warmly, "What are you gentlemen planning to do with your extended leave, hmm? Fishing, skiing, perhaps?"
Whoa.
The rest of the chat is a fine mixture of odd and pleasant, with Victor occasionally dropping some strange, cryptic 1-liners that are meant to be interpreted in a variety of ways, depending on one's point of view.
He does have quite an...obsession with the notions of "rising" and "falling".
Anyways...
Victor Angel now stands in the grand, expansive (and expensive) lobby of the Lancaster-SYS building. Alec and Matt gesture for Victor to pass through his favorite- a beeping, top-of-the-grade body scanner.
Victor calmly walks through it and emerges unscathed on the other side. For a moment, the screen flickers and makes some weird noises, but then the thing just starts beeping again and registers absolutely no abnormalities.
None whatsoever.
Mr. Angel tugs on his suit and wears a smile of pure serenity as he looks upon the sight of the majestic building. The windows, the marble, the interior design, all of it is very beautiful (to him), very high-tech, very professional, very corporate, and very...functional.
The armed security guard gives a firm nod, and the trio make their way to the VIP-only elevator on the far side.
Matt and Alec don't step in.
The latter is gently pushed forward by the former. He forces a smile and says, "This is where we leave you, Mr. Angel. The elevator will take you all the way up to the 36th floor. From there, you can easily find the boardroom. It's...hard to miss."
Matt comments, "They're all waiting for you up there."
Victor puts his hand on the elevator, preventing it from closing, "And privacy is ensured?"
Matt replies, "Oh, yeah. Of course. The boardroom has the most advanced security protocols in the whole building. Soundproof, bulletproof, everything."
Alec chimes in, "Yeah, even a nuke wouldn't be able to put a dent in it. Heh."
Victor shakes his head, "Oh, I would not be so certain of that. Nukes are dangerous things, my friends. Very dangerous and very powerful things. A good day to you both."
The elevator's doors slowly close up and Victor looks down at his reflection in the metallic surface of it. The elevator moves upward with no issues.
Matt blows out a sigh of relief, "Jesus H Christ. What a creep show."
Alec grimaces, "Hmph. C'mon, let's go...take our long-ass holiday a lot earlier. I can't believe that they actually fucking extended it. Who knew?"
The duo start heading back, but something nags at Matt. Victor's whole...vibe, his presence, the way he spoke, all of it seemed so mechanical, so cold, so...odd.
There was an artificial sort of warmth to his smiles. His choice of words was far from typical. He looked so young yet so old at the same time. And yet, he's now meeting with the entirety of the board of this company.
Soon enough, Victor confidently strides into the wide and spacious boardroom. As expected, everybody's here. The Interim CEO sits at the head of the massive black table in the center of the room.
He's a tall African American man in his 50s. He's got a sharp suit on, and his face has the stern look of a ruthless veteran manager/corporate overlord. His arms are crossed and his lips are tightly drawn.
His plaque reads: "Marcellus Baker, Interim CEO."
The approximately 2 dozen board members fall to a dead silence as soon as Vic is inside the room. All murmuring, all conversations, they cease entirely and everybody turns to look at him.
He calmly looks at each and every single person present. A slight smile dances on the edges of his lips as he eyes the Interim CEO and sits himself down opposite him.
A tense pause hangs in the air before he speaks, "My, my, what a pleasure to finally meet all of you in the flesh. Those Internet conference calls, they're so...impersonal, so lacking in...physicality, tangibility, feeling. It's simply a delight to be here with you all."
Marcellus steeples the tips of his fingers together and sits ramrod straight. He regards the client with a careful, steady gaze, "As is it a pleasure for us to finally meet you in-person, Mr. Victor Angel."
Victor nods, "Necessity has brought me here today, at this very spot, at this time of the lovely, sunny day, has it not? A tragedy has taken place, yes."
Jerome reassures the client, "Mr. Angel, any and all security concerns you have are more than safe with us. All that we desire is to hear and discuss the specifics of this grand project that you wish to for us to see through on your behalf, nothing more, nothing less."
Sharla speaks up, "Yes, Mr. Angel. Despite all that has happened, we hope that you will continue to do business with us at Lancaster-SYS."
Victor mirrors the interim CEO's hand gestures by also steepling his fingers, "Yes, indeed, the loss of...3 of your employees and a small child was a tragedy, but you don't seem to be too particularly torn up about it. As for business..."
His gaze darkens a little, "...let us see about that, yes? I'm assuming that the project is nearing completion? Those 3 employees of yours, I was told time and time again, were the best and brightest. Without their presence, my...allies are wondering just how safe things are."
The Interim CEO firmly states, "The project is on track. Jeremy?"
Jeremy clears his throat and smiles professionally, "Y--yes, Mr. Angel. The project is on track. What happened with the 3 employees...and--and the kid was sad, but we have to move on from it."
Victor narrows an eye and leans back in his seat.
Larry jumps in, "Indeed. Mr. Angel, all that we want is for this unfortunate event to not impact the partnership that we have going on. We can't let something like this get in the way of what's good for business."
Victor glares at Larry, "You're not at all sad or broken up about it? 3 of your own, as well as a small 8-year-old child were brutally murdered. My associates, as I am certain that you understand, have been less than thrilled about the company's...response to this whole issue."
Larry stutters, "Um, well, no, no, that's not it. Of--of course, we--we're all sad. I prayed for those 4 poor souls just this week--"
Victor lets out a short chuckle, "You...prayed for them? Is that all you could do? Tell me, Larry...do you even care to know the names of the 3 servitors in your employ or were they just numbers to you people? Statistics and data points on a Cost-Benefit Analysis?"
When mentioning that last part, he looks around the whole room. The board members uncomfortably look down at their fingers.
Sharla quietly replies, "Mr. Angel, all of our employees are valued assets to this company, but what happened had absolutely nothing to do with us. Lancaster-SYS had nothing to do with their deaths."
Mr. Angel sighs deeply and rubs his forehead, "And so that's how things are? Just wash your hands off the heinous killings of...Matteo, Jeong, Jake, and Jake's son...Leo."
Marcellus gets a tad agitated, "Mr. Angel. With all due respect, when you demanded a meeting with the entire Lancaster-SYS board, we were under the assumption that you had something important, something urgent to say, something of note. Are you only here to--"
Victor interjects, "And where is Laurence Lancaster, hmm? I was expecting to see him in that chair, not...you."
Jerome points out, "Mr. Lancaster has been missing. Efforts to recover him are in vain. He was last seen in Magnolia City, but..."
Jeremy cuts in with a deep sigh, "All efforts to contact him or--or find out where he is have failed. There's simply no sign of him anywhere. We have to assume the worst."
Sharla gestures to the interim CEO, "In the meantime, the board came to the decision to appoint an interim CEO until stability can be restored to the company, or 1 of Laurence's sons comes forward and assumes their role as the next CEO."
Larry chuckles, "So far, none of them are cut out for the role, let alone interested. Rest assured, the project is on track and it's going well. There were a few small issues, but that's just...the cost of doing business, Mr. Angel."
Victor takes it all in, thoughtfully and carefully, and his face betrays nothing about what's on his mind. The room falls to silence again, save for the soft humming of the A/C.
After a tense and foreboding silence, Victor wiggles in his seat and his face darkens, "Is...is this how things are now? Business as usual, and 4 souls have been lost because of it."
Marcellus sharply inhales and his jaw clenches up, "Mr. Angel, please tell me that you did not request this meeting just so you can give us some kind of a lecture on our treatment of our employees."
Mr. Angel firmly declares, "So what if I have, hmm? Isn't the customer king and all that? My allies are deeply unsatisfied with the trajectory of your...organization."
Jeremy sputters, "W--what?"
Sharla's face goes red and she looks to Larry, who looks to Jerome.
Marcellus remains composed, "Mr. Angel, with all due respect, you stay in your lane and let us handle things. The project is nearing completion. We've found replacements, and they're hard at work. We have other, bigger issues to deal with."
Victor firmly states, "Oh, believe me, I am deeply, deeply aware of those other 'bigger' issues of yours. Your stock prices aren't doing very well. Your market valuations are falling. There have been whispers about your...investment strategies as of late, as well as talk about..."
He leans forward, "...the way the Lancaster family has been mismanaging things. Those 'bigger' issues, I can assure you that they will all be taken care of."
Marcellus's face tics, "Look...I--I don't know who you think you are, but we are managing just fine. Mr. Lancaster is missing and he hasn't been heard of for quite a while. In his stead, I run the damn show."
Mr. Angel tilts his head to the left, "...Hmmm. And yet, when I look around this cold room, I don't spot a trace of remorse on any of your faces. 3 young men and a small boy are dead, but here you all are...hard at work as you so claim."
Larry frowns deeply and slams the table with a balled fist, "Hey, now! We--we had nothing to do with that!"
Victor clicks his tongue, "Oh, but I believe that you did. Oh, not you specifically, but there is strong suspicion that your...organization, your company had a hand, a very bloody one, in all of this."
Jerome's face scrunches in disbelief, "Wh--what? That's bullshit! I myself conducted an internal investigation and--"
Mr. Angel raises his right index finger in the air, "I'm sure you did, Jerome. I'm sure you did. Pray tell, did you look into the former CEO...or his father for that matter, hmm?"
Gasps of shock and horror come from some of the people around the table.
The interim CEO's eyes bug out, and he nearly falls from his chair.
Jeremy springs up from his seat, "Clarence Lancaster!? What--what's he got to do with any of this?!"
Victor pulls on his shirt sleeves and vaguely answers, "The calculations are still being made. We are still not wholly sure. But what we are sure of is that somebody high up in Lancaster-SYS played a part in the grisly murders."
The boardroom erupts into angry, incredulous shouts. Some people yell about the fact that Vic had no right to come into this room and tell them about this "bullshit", that he's overstepping his bounds, and that he ought to know his place.
Victor doesn't flinch or look at the people angrily yelling at him.
Jerome's nostrils flare and he says, "Mr. Angel, I would advise you to tread very, VERY cautiously. These accusations that you're pulling out of your ass? You better shove 'em back in, fool."
Sharla remarks, "I thought that you were coming all this way to tell us about something that was important."
Victor glares at her and pointedly asks, "Is human life not important? That small boy was gunned down in his own house, surrounded by the blood of his father and his father's co-workers."
He casually goes on, "I have--had a daughter. After certain things, I was...separated from her. She was--is about Leo's age. 8 or 9, or so I should believe. To imagine such a horrendous act being performed upon her, well, I simply could not fathom such a thing."
Jeremy sits his ass back down and gulps, "I--I'm sorry about that, but we--we had nothing to do with what happened to them. You've gotta believe us."
Marcellus stutters, "Laurence--Laurence would never..."
Victor's smile is eerie and menacing, "Hm. Yes. Laurence Lancaster. It is his last name on this dark and vast structure, is it not? I was hoping that he would be in that chair you're currently occupying, Mr. Marcellus. And yet..."
Mr. Angel gets up from his chair, "You have a missing CEO. The timing could not be more...convenient."
Marcellus is getting impatient, "Victor, what the hell is all of this about? What's the meaning of all of this? Who--who the hell are you working for? What--what's this all about?"
Larry chimes in, "Yes, I'm starting to wonder the same exact thing. You--you demanded an in-person meeting with the entirety of the board to speak on something urgent and important, and instead of asking us for an actual project update, you've gone and brought us this nonsense."
Jerome glares baleful daggers at the client, "Whoever your contacts in Lancaster-SYS may be, whatever you're paying for them, this is getting cut off immediately, Mr. Angel. It ends today."
Jeremy lowers his head and says absolutely nothing.
Sharla folds her arms and shoots a very disapproving look at the client, "You had best be taking back what you have said about us having a hand in the tragedy. We have lawyers who could bury you alive. The Lancaster family could even sue you."
Victor Angel doesn't even bother looking at Sharla. All he does is smoothly get up from his seat and climb onto the table with agility and flexibility that don't match up to his apparent age.
All the members of the board gasp in alarm and their eyes bug out as they look up at this...
Angel.
He pulls out 2 silenced pistols from underneath his suit and casually cocks them as he starts walking along the table's length, his shoes loudly clacking against it.
He aims and calmy begins shooting everyone in right in the head. While he walks down the table, he effortlessly squeezes off headshot after headshot, without missing a single step.
It happens so fast that everybody gets killed in the blink of an eye. The suppressed gunshots do not escape the boardroom as blood and bits of brain matter splashes all over the walls, the chairs, and the table.
Marcellus is the last one left alive. Victor shoots him in the chest twice. He falls back and gasps, wheezing in pain as his chest is pierced by the bullets.
The man spits up blood and groans, "He--help."
Still on the table, Victor looks down on the dying interim CEO, "You did have to have your servitors killed. There are far more efficient ways to silence a curious employee."
Marcellus makes a few muffled choking sounds and tries to lift up his hand in a pathetic plea for his life, but his strength gives out.
Victor goes on, "Whether you or anyone else here personally had anything to do with that is, quite frankly, irrelevant. My master has demanded that we tie up any and all loose ends and...I never disobey it."
Mr. Angel coldly cocks both his pistols again and aims them at the interim CEO's head, "You are no longer part of The Grand Equation. Shame. The Fourth Children will assume control of this company, salvage it for parts, and let the rest dissolve."
The faithful Angel of the God-Machine can't help but chuckle a bit, "We have already taken the liberty of giving the rest of your employees a long and extended holiday, which means that your bodies will not be discovered for a very long time."
Marcellus is bleeding from his mouth, and he pleads to be spared, "Please--no. I have...kids. A family, kids--I--I don't--"
Before executing the dying man, Victor proclaims, "What has risen must fall, what has fallen may rise again. The God-Machine lives. The Infrastructure is in place, but we need a few...final components."
With a firm nod, he finishes him off, "Your services are no longer required. I can only hope that the rest of this endeavor goes far better...without your people involved. Goodbye, my good sir."
Marcellus's head is scattered all over the floor. Blood, gore, and bits of brain matter splatters against the floor of the boardroom. Victor puts the safety of the guns on, jumps down from the table, and holsters them in their hiding spots under his suit jacket.
It was nothing short of a miracle that neither the airport scanners nor the scanners at this building picked up his 2 concealed firearms.
The God-Machine works wonders, bends reality (if need be), and performs miracles for its Angels. When the integrity of the Infrastructure is threatened, nothing is off the table.
Mister Victor Angel, the devoted Angel of the God-Machine, takes 1 last and thoughtful (but cold) look at the massacre that he wrought and calmly walks back to his seat.
Smoke rises out of some of the heads. Others are slumped back. Others are laid flat on the table. The once spotless surface of it is now covered with streaks and puddles of red blood.
Some bodies lie dead on the ground, pools of blood beginning to form beneath them. He pulls out a scuffed-looking PDA in his jacket pocket and taps its screen a few times.
The PDA's screen is still blank, but a subsonic humming noise emanates from all over the place. The man gulps a few times and reverentially states, "It is done. All loose ends at Lancaster-SYS have been retired. Laurence was not present. An unexpected deviation."
After a slight pause, he goes on, "All in all, a satisfactory outcome. I understand. What is thy bidding, my master?"
Victor winces slightly while the new orders are being communicated to him, but after about half a minute or so, he firmly nods.
The Angel blinks a few times, "I understand. There's a potential Linchpin at a high-end car dealership. I shall rectify it, my master. The Infrastructure shall remain at a 99.97% integrity level- an optimal level, but there is always room for improvement."
With another slight wince, he goes on, "It shall be done, my master. Yes. Thank you."
A few of the devices of the deceased board members begin to quietly beep and fizz. They come on and display the words: "FALL. RISE. FALL. RISE."
It rapidly repeats, constantly blinking, with no end in sight. The surrounding hum grows louder.
Victor cracks a respectful smile at this and swiftly turns on his heels and walks out of the room with the air of a man with important places to be, importantly vital things to carry out on behalf of a power, a force greater than himself.
Once the Angel of the God-Machine is out of sight and out of mind, the A/C suddenly lowers to a bone-chilling temperature. It's a good way to keep the corpses sorta frozen, because they are NOT gonna be found for a very long time.

She goes on, "And when Luciano and Paul are not having those, they're inflating their egos, deluding themselves, and calling me all sorts of nasty names and then some more."
Clarice calmly asks, "What sort of names?"
"Retard. They love calling me that...and looking at me like I'm some sort of exotic zoo exhibition animal. Even--even though I could easily drain both of 'em in a single night."
"That's truly terrible, my dear. Is there no way out of this ordeal?"
"Not that I know of, no. I'm NOT running again, Clarice. Only now do I kinda realize that the things that Luciano and Paul do...it's so fucked up."
Clarice weakly chuckles, "It's a bit too late for an epiphany when you've been facilitating their lavish lifestyles for quite some time now."
"You got that right. Heh. You got that right. You sound like an old teacher of mine."
"I was a schoolteacher at a public school. And you remind me of many of my old pupils, so bright and full of promise. And yet many of them have been crushed by the weight of the world, the prevailing and pervading socio-economic circumstances constantly at play."
Mauve Lilac lets out a long sigh, "Yeah. I uh...I might have a way out, I dunno. It's kinda risky, but I...kinda um...did something not that long ago. I'm still not so sure WHY I did it, but I did."
"Oh? What did you do?"
Mauve looks her in the eyes and flatly says, "I wrote an anonymous letter to the Securities and Exchange Commission; I tried to keep it as vague as possible. I didn't get a reply back ever since."
It all clicks in Clarice's mind. A smile spreads across her face, "Ah, I see. It all makes perfect sense now. Yes. It all lines up...perfectly, dare I say...beautifully. Finally."
Mauve's face scrunches up in confusion, "What do you mean???"
"I have no idea why you did it, and it doesn't really matter at this moment. Rest assured, your letter has not gone unanswered. There is an SEC employee currently in this Domain and he is investigating your...benefactors."
Mauve gasps and can hardly believe what she's hearing! Is this it? Is her salvation, her way out, finally coming?
"You can't be serious! This is it? This is really it!? Well, who is it? WHO IS HE? C'mon, Clarice! PLEASE! Tell me!" the desperation (mixed in with traces of joy) is quite apparent in Mauve's tone of voice.
Clarice doesn't necessarily share in the joy, "It's not as straightforward and clear-cut as you were hoping, dearie. Markham Reed is not just an SEC employee. He's also Kindred, like us. However, he is not of our Clan."
Mauve's expression shifts, the last bits of hope dashed away or banished from her face, "Is the SEC gonna bring the hammer down on the 2 of 'em?"
"I do not know. I simply do not know. Regardless, this Markham Reed seems like a decent fellow."
"Can I meet him, give him my testimony...try to cut out some kind of a deal--"
Clarice cuts her off, "You should be aware that your 'benevolent' benefactors have tried to have Mr. Reed killed. As far as I can tell, the killers have failed in their attempts. Yet I fear that the worst has not fully passed."
Mauve covers her mouth with her trembling hands, "Oh my god. Oh...oh my god. They--they're murderers! At--attempted murder! Is Markham OK?!"
"Last I checked- which wasn't that long ago- he seemed to be faring well. Then again, those who mean ill towards him are far from giving up."
"Oh god. Should I do anything from my--"
Clarice advises her fellow Malkavian, "The best that you can do is play along...and wait. Just...wait for Markham Reed to come to you. I'll tell him about you when the time's right and send him your way. Yes?"
Mauve nods, "Yes. And how long is that gonna take? I--I can't go on like this. It's...it's so goddamn wrong and unethical and--I got no other choice; I just have to force myself to play along, manipulating the numbers in my head and..."
Clarice interjects, "Just breathe, dear. It will all come to a head eventually, I'm sure. Your ordeal is soon coming to an end..."
Mauve's smile barely reaches up to her eyes, "I hope so."
Clarice, with a gentle and sincere tone, says, "Good luck, dear. This is where our conversation concludes. I can feel my Ghoul, Arnold, starting to worry about me."
"Oh. Um...Clarice?"
"Yes?"
Mauve looks up at the gigantic yellow bird and queries, "This big bird creepily staring at us...what is it?"
Clarice looks up at it as well and shrugs, "Likely an effect of our shared Dementation. The Malkavian Madness Network, on the best of nights, defies most laws of reality, physics, and sanity."
She looks at Mauve, who is now smiling, and says, "Oh. Just...don't worry too much about it. Hold out until we can put an end to all this. I promise you that."
Mauve Lilac nods, "OK. Why can't you just tell this Markham guy to meet me tonight?"
Clarice shakes her head, "It's too risky. There are some very dangerous people after him. And we also cannot risk your 'beloved' bosses finding out. They do go to great lengths to keep you a secret, hmm?"
"Yep, they sure do. I--I can't believe that they'd be so brazen and reckless and try to...have someone killed!"
"Then, we ought to thank our lucky stars that Markham Reed is no ordinary mortal. Then again, every person has their limits. Kindred are most certainly no exception."
"If you say so, Clarice. Listen, it was really nice talking to you and--"
Just then, seemingly out of nowhere, a large snake-like creature made of hardened metal violently emerges from the raging "waters" all around the duo.
Circuitry and wiring wraps around its length, its mouth is wide open, and its many uneven eyes are like flickering computer screens. Its sharp teeth make a whirring, clicking sound.
Clarice is shocked, but Mauve is stunned. Her eyes are as wide as dinner plates, and she lets out a shrill cry of horror and shock!
Mauve shrieks in fear and leaps behind the Primogen, her face painted with an absolutely terrified expression.
Clarice grabs a hold of her and holds her close, "Easy, easy, my dear. What's--what's gotten into you?"
Mauve points to the rapidly approaching entity, but Clarice sees nothing when her eyes follow the other Seer's shivering finger.
Clarice takes a few steps forward, and her eyes widen as she is hit by an ultrasonic wave that brings her to her knees.
"AAAAAAH!"
The creature relentlessly plunges forward and devours Mauve whole, dragging her away into a place unknown, unsearchable.
Mauve struggles and fights, trying her hardest to escape this hellish nightmare. Back in her bedroom, she springs up, covered in a thin film of cold, bloody sweat.
She lets out a terrified squeak, her shoulders rising up to her ears and her mouth wide open. Ms. Lilac checks herself, patting her face and her body to ensure that she's still intact.
Once she's satisfied that she's all in 1 piece, she buries her face in her hands and begins sobbing.
Clarice, meanwhile, stirs awake back in her Haven. That high-pitched sonic attack still lingers in her throbbing skull, but she's a bit more coherent.
She blinks a few times, looking around. The Malk Primogen takes a moment to gather her composure.
After a short while, Arnold knocks on the door and worriedly calls out, "Teacher? Clarice? Are you still in there? Are you still sleeping? Are you gonna bite me if I come in?"
