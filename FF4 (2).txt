Chapter 28: Hunters
The sun? It rises and it sets at the appointed time. The city chugs along with the usual motions- work, play, study, surviving (barely), thriving (only a privileged few), and everything in between.
Once the sun has fully disappeared from view, the moon above shines, its silvery rays casting an almost mystical glow on the city as a whole.
Farah, the Imbued Hunter, lives in a small, semi-shoebox style apartment in Al-Kushraf.
She is 30-something, is of Middle Eastern descent, has caramel skin, a messy mane of black curly hair, and a rugged face with a few battle scars (some fresher than others).
The Hunter wears an orange jacket- it's very bright and colorful; a white stripe runs down the sleeves. She also wears a pair of dark blue jeans and black boots.
For now, she prepares for the night to come. Officially, she hops from job to job (just like she hops from shitty place to shittier place) but unofficially, she's a monster hunter extraordinaire.
She's been doing this since she was in her late teens, and she's not about to stop anytime soon. Her Imbuing involved some kind of cryptic pamphlet that she was fixated on for weeks on end.
It was only after her 1st successful kill (a Risen zombie) that she understood what she was, what her purpose was. Ever since then, she has relentlessly hunted the many entities that go bump in the night.
She doesn't discriminate- vampires, werewolves, zombies, and other supernaturals have all been on the receiving end of her righteous fury.
When it comes to Edges, she has a trusty MP5, leadership skills, and contacts amongst the Desperation-stricken lower echelons of the mortal populace who are eager to fight back.
(Or gain some semblance of control over their messy lives).
Right now, she does some freelance footwork for a local company. She gets paid a pittance for whatever she's told to do, but it's enough to keep her fed and watered.
Her economic situation, like many in this world of ours, is bordering on precarity. She barely makes ends meet, but she manages to get by somehow.
After her old Cell was wiped out back in the US of A, she booked it to the UAE and unwittingly became a pawn in Seth Steale's plans to rid Dubai of its Kindred presence.
Farah was the one who effectively brought Final Death upon Prince Angela Whiteford of Clan Gangrel, and she (along with her Cellies) is also the reason why most of the other survivors have been forced to flee the city.
That was mere weeks ago, but it feels like an eternity has passed since then.
Farah throws herself on her patchy single-seater couch and blows a raspberry. She wiggles until she finds a comfortable position and looks up at the ceiling. She sighs and says to herself, "Ugh..."
She throws her feet up on the round table, which is ridden with papers, documents, magazines, and various other paraphernalia. She grabs a random magazine and flips through it.
She rolls her eyes, groans, and tosses it on the floor. She picks up another one and reads it from cover to cover. She yawns, throws it on the floor, and goes for another one.
Farah gets up to pull her thin curtains closed. She also double-checks that her front door is locked. She secures the numerous deadbolts and goes back to her seat. She turns on the TV and watches some news.
She lets out a deep breath and takes her phone out- it's a bulky touchscreen phone, but the screen is horrendously cracked, and the battery life is waning.
It's on its way to being old and outdated, but it still works well enough for her needs.
She sends a quick text to her fellow Cellies in a group chat: "hey, salam everyone. i got some new info from scaly. Just waiting 4 confirmation and then we are a go. Get ready, we r going Hunting 2nite!"
Farah follows it up with a few grinning and flexed biceps emojis.
In under a minute, the replies pour in. It's total enthusiasm and support from all of the group chat members. Farah weakly grins and nods to herself.
Her thumbs dance across the cracked screen: "great, I'm glad that we r all in agreement. 2nite, we begin the Reckoning of Al-Kushraf, UAE!"
After that, she pulls her aged Toshiba laptop towards her and jabs her index finger on the power button. She waits for it to boot up. She logs into her favorite website (which one is plainly obvious) and licks her chapped lips in waiting anticipation.
She types out a PM to Seth but decides against sending it. She doesn't wanna give the impression of being clingy or far too Desperate. She opens another tab on her browser.
Farah browses through her old emails, checking to see if there are any new messages from her friends and family back home in Iran. She was born and raised in America and has never actually been to the country of her parents.
She has always wanted to go and see it for herself, but she's had neither the time nor the money to do so. Both her parents have pretty mixed and complex feelings about Iran- understandably so.
With all that is going on in the world, the Hunter just hopes that there will be an Iran left for her to visit someday, maybe not now, but soon (when she's probably got enough saved up). She sighs and shakes her head.
After a few dry minutes, she closes the job searching website and goes back to the other tab. And soon enough- as expected- Seth sends her a message. She lightly smirks and begins typing back.
(For all intents and purposes, Farah is not a bad or vile person- she is a Desperate person. And Desperate Hunters do Desperate things for a variety of reasons...)
Elsewhere in this gilded cage of a city...
Aaron Hart is up and about, but still severely weakened. What has happened to him is still clearly taking its toll on him. At least he now is able to walk to and from the bathroom without falling. That is progress.
He's resting on his bed when Svetlana and John grab a seat on either side of him. The 2 Assamites fix a gaze of worry upon him and his broken, fragile form.
They know that he has been through some serious shit down in that torture chamber. Aaron lightly turns his head to glance at Svetlana and John and he sighs deeply, "Thank you..." he croaks.
Svetlana pats his hand, "Is no problem."
John chimes in, "Hey, you're welcome. So, uh...you ready to talk to us or do you, like, need more time to sort through things?"
Aaron nods slowly, "No, no...it's fine. I--I'm kinda going nuts being alone with my thoughts here, heh. I--I mean, don't get me wrong. This fancy room and this fancy hotel that I'm staying in is--is great. Really. Thank you."
His words come out like a babbling brook. But the message is loud and clear.
John shrugs and states, "You can thank Seth for...arranging all of this for you."
Aaron blinks rapidly, "Oh, su...sure."
Svetlana queries, "Do you remember anything from your time in captivity?"
Aaron's lips quiver and he lets out a shuddered gasp, "Ye--yeah. 'Course I do. It was just as bad as you could imagine, err...your name? Sorry, it's been a helluva few weeks..." Aaron meekly says.
Still in a sort of daze due to all the medication in his body, he reintroduces himself, "I am Aaron Hart. Human rights lawyer from Brooklyn. Eh...yeah."
"Svetlana Bobrov. I am from Ukraine SSR."
"John Suarez. Magnolia City."
Aaron takes a lot of effort to turn himself over, so that he faces John. He's got his back propped up by pillows. Before turning, his gaze lingers on Svetlana for a bit, "Y...you look kinda pale. You doing OK?"
Svetlana tucks some of her hair behind her ear and smiles, "I am fine. It is you we should be concerned about. How YOU are feeling?"
Aaron takes a few deep breaths, "Getting better. So, Mr. Suarez--"
John holds up a hand, "John's fine. Seriously. Don't be so formal, it makes me feel...old. Heh. And I'm already pretty damn old, haha!"
Aaron gulps and weakly grins, "Yeah, haha...yeah..." his words come out slowly, each one taking its own sweet time.
He turns his gaze to the ceiling of his hotel suite and sharply exhales, "So, when--when I was down there, those...fucking maniacs...they asked me a lotta questions about some pretty strange shit..."
John asks, "What kinds of questions? Do you remember anything at all?"
Aaron responds, "Y--yeah, I...they asked me if I had any connections to any so-called 'blank bodies'. Truth be told, I haven't heard of that term, like, ever. Then, those bastards told me it was their special top-secret ways of--of referring to--"
He momentarily pauses as his eyes begin to well up. He turns his head to his right and he faces Svetlana, "--to vampires."
Svetlana's eyes flit over to John's- the message is pretty damn clear. Her brow creases and her eyes narrow. She lightly pats Aaron's hand, "Did you know anything about it before you came to UAE?"
Aaron shakes his head slowly, "Nope. I live and work in New York. I see all sorts of um...strange things. But--but vampires?"
The mortal lazily waves his hand in a dismissive gesture, "I mean, c'mon. That's really stretching it, heh. I've never believed in such things before, like, ever."
Svetlana leans back in her chair, "And why exactly were you coming to UAE for? Holiday or something else?"
Aaron's chuckle is hoarse, "Holiday? Me? Hah! Nah, I came here for work. But that all went down the toilet when I was kidnapped by those psychos in the airport. Hmph."
John clears his throat, "A human rights lawyer coming to the UAE for work. Yeah, it figures. This fucking place...it's pretty messed up. And that's me putting it pretty mildly."
Aaron wipes his nose with a Kleenex and tosses the soggy paper in an open waste basket nearby. He leans forward in bed and rasps, "Who the fuck were those people anyway, huh?"
John shrugs, "Israeli spec ops, I'd guess. Kinda, but also worse in every conceivable way imaginable."
The mortal's eyebrows go up a bit, "And why the hell would THEY be interested in finding vampires in the UAE? A--assuming vampires are even real and the whole thing isn't a massive pretext for some kinda crazy, illegal military op or whatever?"
Svetlana gives John a worried glance and tells the human rights lawyer, "Is complicated situation. And we are sorry that you were dragged into this."
Aaron mumbles, "I still don't get it. Like when--when I was down there, being tortured by that maniac, he kept asking if I knew about the blank body named...John Suarez."
The mortal fixes his unsteady gaze on the speedy Assamite in question, "Yeah. He--he even strongly implied that...that you're a...vampire. But you're not a vampire be--because vampires simply don't exist."
Svetlana states in an unflustered tone of voice, "John, we should tell him, no? Is right thing to do. He has suffered too much already. He deserves to know the truth. But is your decision."
Aaron blinks repeatedly at the stealthy Assamite's words. He groans while turning towards the ceiling. He sighs and waits for a reply, his mouth slightly open as he wheezes and breathes.
John looks at his scuffed shoes and bites his lower lip. There is still the Masquerade to consider, even if it is not necessarily enforced in this Prince-less/Sheriff-less Domain. Still, he feels uneasy about spilling the beans to this random stranger.
He glances towards his Childe and says, "It's your call, Svetlana. If you feel that he should know everything- no holding back- then go ahead. If not, then that's fine by me too."
Svetlana looks on the broken mortal with pity in her dead eyes. She sees how hollow his cheeks look, how his eyes have sunken deep into their sockets, how pale his face is- it's like the very life has been sapped from him.
She clenches her fist and resolves to tell him everything, "John and I, we are vampires."
John's eyes widen at how his Childe opted to come out swinging, "Whoa. Uh..."
Svetlana explains to a drowsy Aaron, "This is what that Israeli madman was referring to, Aaron. And somehow, you got caught up in our...world. We are sorry for what you had to go through."
Aaron slowly turns to Svetlana and blankly stares at her. His jaw is slightly agape, "So--so, it's all real, then? You--you're not just trying to pull my leg or play some really elaborate prank or something, huh?"
Svetlana dryly responds, "Nyet, this is not prank. We are Kindred. Me and John belong to a Clan known as Banu Haqim. I was Embraced 5 years ago and John was...Embraced in 1960s."
Aaron's eyes widen to an alarming degree. He turns over to look at John. He exhales deeply and chuckles to himself, "You--you're from the 1960s????"
John shrugs, "Yep. Been there, lived through it all, saw it all firsthand back in Magnolia City..." he pauses to laugh, "I...I still distinctly remember all of it- it's so vividly fresh and clear in my head, heh."
The mortal covers his mouth with his hand and is stunned into silence for a few moments. He gulps, "This is all so fucking trippy..." he weakly mumbles.
His mind is far too disoriented and now he's overflowing with too many questions to focus on any 1 in particular. The sheer magnitude of the situation at hand is nothing short of mind-blowing.
He does ask, "So, why the hell are Israeli spec ops hunting you people down? And what are you even doing in the UAE?"
John answers, "Well, that's a story in and of itself. All you need to know is that they're probably part of a bigger organization that us Kindred refer to as the Second Inquisition. They hunt our kind down with ruthless precision, and they're pretty much all over the world now."
Aaron's eyebrows furrow as he rubs his chin with his left hand, "Hmm...sounds nuts."
Svetlana simply says, "It is the truth. We do what we must to survive. We drink blood and are only active at nights. During day, we sleep so our bodies can rest. We also do not age."
John interjects, "That's--that's how the vampire myths came to be in the first place- it's based on truths and also lies and a hell lotta gray areas, and there's also our whole Masquerade, which basically means keeping our existence a secret from, well...people like you."
The human rights lawyer softly says, "Well, ain't much of a secret now, huh? Why--why are you telling me all of this? You're not, like, gonna try and Men-In-Black my mind after you've spilled your dark secrets to me, right? Because...well...that's some messed up shit."
Svetlana shakes her head, "Nyet. Of course not. You have already suffered greatly, Aaron. We do not wish to add to your pain. That is why you should know the truth of what you have been caught up in."
The mortal gulps and lets out a hoarse gasp, "O--OK. That's pretty thoughtful of you, I guess. And can either of you, like, turn into mist or bats or whatever?"
John chuckles at that, "Yeah, that's actually kinda possible, but that's not a Discipline that the Banu Haqim Clan has access to."
Aaron mutters, "Ah, OK. That--that's neat. Well, what can you do that a guy like me can't?"
John matter-of-factly answers, "I can go pretty fast. We call that Celerity."
Svetlana chimes in, "Da. I can disappear from plain sight. That is called Obfuscate."
Aaron lets out a dry chuckle, "Cool, cool. Rad, even. Heh. And the whole blood thing? That sounds kinda yikes to me. Can you, like, not do it or try to maybe...I dunno, drink from animals or buy it from a butcher or something?"
John grimly replies, "Not drinking blood would be far worse for the people around us. It's a real struggle, believe me. But we're able to keep our Beasts in check. Most of the time, anyway. I can't really speak for anybody else in that regard."
Svetlana interjects, "I understand that it is not a pretty thing which we do when we drink blood, but there is, for us, no other choice. Either we must address our Hunger or we will Frenzy. And this can cause great problems for all of us."
Aaron simply cannot believe what he is hearing. The mortal clumsily rolls over and remarks, "Holy shit. Now--now that I think about it, there are certain things which I saw back in NYC that are kinda starting to make sense. I--I guess."
Svetlana leans closer and inquires, "Like?"
The mortal tiredly answers, "There was this chick who I went on a date with. She barely ate, almost like the food itself was foreign to her...if--if that makes any goddamn sense."
He blinks rapidly and goes on, "She was pale as a washed sheet. And here's the weirdest part- when I asked for a selfie, she absolutely refused. She told me that she didn't quite get along with cameras."
John opines, "Yep, sounds like a vampire, alright."
"Da."
Aaron blows a raspberry, "Wow. This is crazy, huh?"
John proceeds to query, "What happened with that date of yours, anyway?"
Aaron weakly shrugs, "Eh, human rights lawyers don't exactly get much in the way of action. We kinda nodded at each other and went our separate ways after a walk through Central Park. She kept going on and on about how everything's changed, like it was a bad thing or something."
He gulps, "Now I think I kinda know what she meant. Are all vampires sorta stuck in the past, or was it just her?"
John plainly states, "I think I've adapted pretty damn well, actually. Again, everyone's different. Diff'rent Strokes, remember that?"
Svetlana adds, "Da. For me, it has not been very long, but I believe I have done good job adjusting."
Aaron sniffs and remarks, "Huh. I guess so. So, you retain a part of yourselves after you become vampires?"
Svetlana tells him, "Our Humanity is the most important thing of all to us. John here is the one who Embraced me...saved me." For a moment, she stares off into the far distance with an unreadable expression.
Then she returns to the here-and-now, "He taught to me how to control the Beast, how to survive each night. To him, I owe everything."
John rubs the back of his head and quietly mumbles, "Aw shucks, thanks. And I owe you just as much, Svetlana."
Aaron remarks, "Wow. That's wild."
Svetlana concludes, "Yes. Very much so. But that is only the surface, there is so much more. Still, I think it is better for you to rest now, hmm?"
Aaron mumbles, "It's like a whole world out there, huh?"
John snickers, "Heh, yeah. A world of darkness. And intrigue, secrets, danger, and also of wonders that you can barely begin to imagine, Aaron. There are far worse things in this world than us. But you already know that, don't you?"
Aaron's expression turns sour, "Like those--like those Israeli maniacs who kidnapped me and--and tortured me. Yeah, those guys were total monsters, right? I guess you have it rough out there, huh?"
Svetlana empathetically tells the mortal, "We are sorry that you got involved in all of this. It was not meant to be like this. We are sorry for...making mess of your life by involving you in all of our problems."
The mortal sighs and mumbles, "Well, I don't really hold it against you. Or--or John over here. Really, I don't. I'm just glad that you were both straight with me- that's a rarity on its own."
Aaron's eyelids slowly fall and close as the medication takes hold, "And you're not, like, planning to suck my blood or anything, right?"
John shakes his head, "Absolutely not. We already had our dinner."
Aaron lets out a light gasp at the bluntness of it all, "Oh...wow. Yeah, that--that makes sense. Wow. Rad, huh? And you expect me to keep all of this a secret, right? Haha, any NDAs that I have to sign or anything? I mean...I guess so, huh?"
Svetlana dryly responds, "We trust you enough not to do anything...stupid. You have suffered enough already- too much, more than any human being can bear. And...well...we feel guilty that you are mixed up in this, even if we did not directly put you in trouble."
John scratches his palm, "Yep, what she said. It's not like you uh...have anyone to tell, right?"
Aaron sighs deeply, "Apart from Mr. Stone at the law firm and a few close friends and family members, I--no, I won't tell anyone. I know when to keep quiet about important matters, y'know? I know my limits."
He muses, "Y'know, when I came to the UAE, I thought that- by now- I'd have concluded my work here, be done with it, and get back home to good ol' NYC, heh. How wrong I was, huh?"
Svetlana pensively lowers her head and says nothing.
John presses his lips into a thin line, "What were you planning on doing here, anyway?"
Aaron props himself up on the bed as the meds continue to take hold. His voice begins to sound sleepy as he rasps, "I'm a human rights lawyer. Somebody tipped us off to some pretty nasty human rights violations taking place in Al-Kushraf, UAE."
He takes a light sip of water and proceeds, "So, the firm that I work for sent me out here to help set up the case. I was supposed to work in partnership with some of the local advocates and lawyers to investigate and uncover the truth behind these terrible abuses."
The torture victim bitterly concludes, "But things didn't exactly...pan out that way. Now, I'm just too damn afraid to call up Mr. Stone and let him know how I bungled everything up- people were relying on us because we're often their last, best hope...y'know?"
John pats Aaron on his shoulder, "Don't go too hard on yourself, buddy. You came with the best of intentions, but you're a human being just like everyone else. We all have our limits. Besides, there's still a lot more out there for you to do, right?"
Aaron asks the 2 Assamites, "Do you think that- once I'm fully better, recovered, healed up, all that jazz- I'll be able to go back to work with a clean conscience?"
John takes his hand off of the mortal, "Maybe? Probably? Why?"
Aaron huffs a sigh, "I was supposed to make name partner at the law firm I work at- VNS- before this year ended. Now, I dunno if I can even make it to the fucking bathroom without falling down and--and having a mental breakdown."
John and Svetlana remain silent- this is a man in obvious physical and mental agony.
John clenches his fist and thinks to himself, "Those goddamn Israeli bastards. What the fuck did they do to him down there? He--he had literally nothing to do with us and--and they just went at him like he was their punching bag. Tch."
Aaron wistfully says, "They'd have called it VNSH. The 'H' being for Hart, that's me. Heh. Or that was me." He softly laughs as his eyes flutter and close shut.
John suggests, "Maybe we should let you rest, hmm?"
Aaron slurs his words and waves his hands about, "Ah, no, no. Stay. Talk to me. These past few goddamn days and nights have been the most trying in all my life...the silence is really not helping- even law school and subsisting on canned beans wasn't this tough. I'd like the company, even if it is 2 vampires."
If he says so. As such, the 2 Blade of Haqim Neonates spend a few more minutes conversing with Aaron Hart in his (fully paid for) hotel suite, about (Un)life, politics, law, justice, and whatever else comes to mind.
In the midst of the talk, Aaron groans and says, "I always strongly believed that--that justice waits for no man and--and I should strive to help deliver justice for those in need of it, because--because that's the right thing to do. And you Banu Haqim seem to have the same aims as I do..."
He pauses and goes on, "...even if we may disagree on the methods of delivering that final verdict, heh."
The conversation draws on until the duo finally take their leave. All in all, it was a rather enjoyable one. John and Svetlana head back out the way that they came.
Besides, Aaron looks really sleepy and needs his rest more than anything.
John remarks, "Well, that went fairly well. Svetlana?"
Svetlana presses the elevator button, "Hmm?"
"Did we make the right call in revealing our true nature to him?"
"It was right thing to do, John. I know that we must uphold Masquerade, but Aaron suffered because of us. And he is not fool- I know that he is smart man who will not jeopardize our safety."
"Yep. I do hope that's the case, Svetlana. If anybody found out..." John trails off, letting that unspoken possibility hang in the air for a few moments.
He then adds, "Anyway, do you think that he'd be able to uh...get back on his feet and go back to his life back in the Big Apple when he's better?"
Svetlana responds as the elevator doors open in front of her and her Sire, "That...I do not know if I can answer. He has seen too much already. For him to simply go back 'to normal' and forget all of what happened to him here- this will not likely happen, nyet. I do not think so."
John shoves his hands into his pockets, "Yeah, I figured as much."
The 2 Assassins get in the empty elevator and prepare for the long descent back down to the ground floor.
On the way down, John ponders, "Hmm. So, ready for tonight? Yet another fight. I just hope that we don't have to see that damn helicopter again."
"Da. I am always ready. You?"
John reminds his Childe, "It's just 1 battle after another. We take it easy, do what we can to whittle their forces down, and...when- or if- we're ready to hit their HQ, we'll make sure to send a big message that they won't be forgetting anytime soon. One that nobody can ignore."
Miss Bobrov softly smiles, "Da. 1 battle after another, we Judge them all until we no longer have to fight."
Mr. Suarez turns his gaze heavenwards, "Yep. And when that all blows over, we hope for the best...whatever that entails, heh."
Out of concern, Svetlana asks, "And what happens next, will it be as you say...the best? Or will we be forced to run again?"
"Al-Kushraf is- for better or worse- our home now. We can't go back to Dubai after what happened back there, we really can't. The Israelis have proven themselves to be pretty fucking ruthless. But at the very least, I believe that we've thinned the herd enough."
"Hmm. Da. I still worry, John. I worry about what comes next."
"Yeah. Me too, Svetlana. Me too."
(They had no idea how terribly bad it was gonna get.)
Whilst they were talking, Aaron told the 2 vampires that he simply has no capability of doing what he came here to do (he was, understandably, torn up about this).
They tried their best to reassure him. He was, at the very least, grateful that they came clean to him about their true natures as nocturnal predators, and he has agreed to not tell anybody else about this- they can both trust him with that.
(Hopefully.)

Aaron, for his part, is kinda stoked that he's effectively getting a free stay at The Grand Alhambra. It's no cheap place to stay at for extended periods of time, but Seth is footing the bill.
However, Mr. Steale doesn't do these kinds of things out of the magnanimity of his dead heart. He probably has his own intentions with the mortal human rights lawyer.
What those are (if they're anything at all) remains to be seen in the coming nights.
A little later...
Tonight's battle against the Zionist scum takes place near the outskirts of the city. A large truck rammed into a fleet of unmarked SUVs, which then crashed into the side of a large warehouse building.
The Unit 8211 raid teams were in those SUVs. Most of them were, thankfully, crushed and killed upon impact. Others were able to escape the carnage.
Smoking and smashed-up vehicles litter the asphalt as John and Svetlana rush up towards the scene, their footsteps crunching on the broken glass and spilled fuel.
The warehouse in question is extremely large...and extremely empty. It's a fairly sturdy structure with several windows on all sides. However, most of those windows are either barred or have iron grills on them.
Some moss and overgrowth can be spotted on the exterior walls. This warehouse is, essentially, abandoned. Or, at the very least, mostly so. It's been slated for demolition, but bureaucracy (even in the fuckin' UAE) means that the place won't be touched for another few years or so.
(And that's not taking into account all the red tape involved).
The surviving members of the raid teams fall back into the warehouse proper whilst the Kindred give chase. The warehouse is, naturally, a maze of stacked boxes and dusty shelves. The air is dry, musty, and stale.
Zeina is busy battling another squad on the other side of the city with a few other Assamites. That leaves John, Svetlana, and a mix of Blade and non-Blade Assamite alike to deal with the Israelis.
John readies his katana and charges into the fray of gunfire, flames, bullets, fists, screams, and wooden stakes. He moves like water as he dodges and weaves amongst the chaos.
1 Zionist operative is impaled through the back and has his spine viciously severed by John. He collapses to the ground and dies, blood gushing out of the fatal wound.
His partner opens fire with their fiery shotgun, but the barrage is swiftly dodged by John's Celerity. He whooshes around, hisses, and closes in on the assailant. Their arm is sliced clean off. Then their neck is split wide open.
Svetlana struggles while a stake-wielding operator pins her down from the front. She grunts and struggles to keep that damned piece of wood from entering her sternum. She's too busy focusing on that to actually attack her foe. She lets out a pained snarl.
The fascists seem a lot stronger and even more enraged than ever. A few Assamites lie still on the warehouse floor, their corpses reduced to ashes in mere moments.
Svetlana's eyes widen as she gives it her all, yet her limbs shake and her arms begin to ache and strain under the immense force exerted upon her by this stake-wielding maniac.
She ferociously growls and pushes even harder. Sweat beads down her trimmed brow. She grits her teeth and pushes against Svetlana's defenses, trying her absolute damnedest to force the stake through her chest.
This is not looking good for the Assamite Neonate.
The other Assamites either slash, stab, and tear the Inquisitors to pieces, or turn their own dreadful weaponry against them. This is mostly done by the non-Blade Assamites because those who are of the Blade of Haqim strongly believe that fighting with guns is quite dishonorable.
Regardless, when in the midst of such heated battle, such notions tend to take a back seat. When Final Death stares one down in the face, honor and dignity are no longer important. Only survival matters. That, and vengeance.
And so, some Assamites decide to use the tools of their oppressors. An Assamite picks up a gore-covered SMG and wildly fires it into the enemy ranks, mowing them down as the bullets spray out in a hail of hot AP lead.
A few of the troops are cut down and riddled with a hail of gunfire. Svetlana sees this as an opening. While her foe is distracted, she pushes her off of her.
Svetlana then kicks her foe hard in the midsection, knocking her onto her back. From there, the Assamite pounces on the enemy and drives BOTH of her daggers straight into the woman's skull.
