Chapter 21: Rescue
The sun rises over the land. After last night's fierce fighting, the vampires scattered back to their Havens to rest, mend their wounds, recuperate, and prepare for whatever comes next.
There is not much room for respite in this struggle, however. No time for a vacation, to put it bluntly. During the day, the Kindred are vulnerable- completely still and prone to attack.
The immortal (but not indestructible) beings of the dark night succumb to their day-sleep as the sun rises, and the skies burn bright with the rays of life (and light).
Just like they did for Abdullah, the Zionist thugs record videos of a few captured Assamites being obliterated by the rising sun. The videos, somehow, find their way onto the systems of the Nosferatu.
(It's going to be quite a nasty waking-up surprise).
Those videos are sent as a warning of what will happen to the Kindred in this part of the world. Lt. Raz herself edited the video and put it together after the recording equipment was brought back to the main base.
She has a callous and indifferent reaction to seeing captured Kindred writhing in agony and burning to ashes. She looks over at a few of her fellow soldiers and sees that they all feel the same way.
Some even gleefully chuckle and high-five each other.
Like last time, she has the video end with big, bold white text on a black background that reads: "AM YISRAEL CHAI".
Adding insult to injury, Lt. Liora Raz added some kind of dark synth pop music to the grim video before sending it off.
That's Unit 8211's way of doing PSYOPS and sending a message to the Kindred that they're not backing down anytime soon.
The day goes on with the mortal denizens of the city remaining none the wiser. Panic and fear set in once the papers and other media outlets start running with the whole "gang" narrative.
Either way, the Emirati authorities are hellbent on reassuring everybody that Al-Kushraf city is still THE place to come shop, set up a business, shit like that.
The families of the emergency personnel who were killed by the Israelis last night are given generous "compensations". Basically, a shut the hell up and deal with it kind of deal.
Not every family member is willing to accept that, however.
1 man, whose firefighter son was gunned down by the Zionists, is seen crying as he walks away from a reporter. He wants answers, not dirhams.
Absalom, the Malkavian Primogen, is still in the throes of the day-sleep. The sun slowly descends upon the land. The Kindred in this Middle Eastern metropolis are already preparing to stir awake.
The Elder Malk can feel his entire mind being yanked into the inescapable depths of the Malkavian Madness Network. He finds himself unable to move, unable to speak, unable to do anything.
Creepy carnival music plays all around him. Colors and shapes dance about him and mock him. His mouth opens and closes, but he cannot even scream. He cannot speak.
His limbs are stiff and unmoving. He cannot feel them. Absalom can't move a single digit or twitch a single facial muscle.
The voices swirl all around him.
"This is a fucking travesty! It's depraved, disgusting, immoral, and unsanitary!"
"Agh! Can't you see that I'm in here? I finally...finally get some alone time. HEY! STOP! What are you doing?!"
Some hoarse-voiced woman calls out, "I can make my own fairy book ending."
"BITCHES!"
The Elder grits his teeth and pushes all of his mental strength into trying to break free, but it's not enough.
A gentle, barely audible call tickles the Elder's ears, "Absalom. Absalom. Hey, it's me."
Absalom's lips quiver, "Ma--Madeline?"
"Y--yeah. Just do what you did last time. Easy steps, now."
"But--but--I cannot remember...I--"
"Do it. You know the words; you know the dance. You know what to do."
"I..."
Madeline's voice takes on a more synthetic, computerized tone, "Absalom. Do. It."
"I cannot!" the Elder Malkavian cries out in near pain.
The voices laugh maniacally and directly taunt Absalom, "Aww, it's the feudal lord. Look at him struggling. Dumb son of a bitch. He was a fucking tyrant in his time. Serves him right."
Madeline sighs softly. Beeping and whirring noises follow, "Absalom, focus. Focus."
Absalom's mind immediately goes back to his past. It was literal centuries ago.
Even to this very night, he vividly remembers it all. While others might've chosen to forget, Absalom has chosen to remember.
It was a hot summer day during the Middle Ages, and not all were having a good time. The peasants were not so happy about the latest order of their lord.
Absalom, who went by a different name back then (obviously), had ordered the serfs to work till the end of the day. Severe beatings would await those who disobeyed or defied the benevolence of the then-mortal Absalom.
Absalom and his large family would have a loud celebration. Wine goblets clinking, sparkling red wine flowing like water itself.
In the middle of the revelry, Absalom would rise up, his healthy face flushed with wine, and proudly declare, "Set thine eyes upon mine lands! Behold my vast riches and wealth!"
A ragged looking man with a scraggly gray beard would often roam Absalom's vast estate. He was only ever seen at nights and was constantly drooling.
He spoke in grunts and growls, barely forming cogent words. Even the peasants stayed away from him and chided their children to AVOID going out during the night.
Absalom had a large portrait of himself hung up in the main hall. The portrait was painted by a skilled artist of the time, and it was said to have captured the Absalom's visage in the finest of details.
His piercing blue eyes, his broad shoulders, and his long, flowing black hair.
The scraggly man was suspected of being a runaway from a local asylum. He would often go around town, and the locals would shun him as well.
Absalom would try- time and again- to get this annoyance off his land, but that man always had a way of giving the well-armed guards the slip. There were even times when Absalom's children would see that same man standing over their beds in the dead of the night.
He would just stare at them, growling softly.
It got so bad that 1 night, Absalom himself decided to find this fellow and get rid of him. Permanently. Personally. No guards, no help from any virile showoff cousins, just him and a shiny sword that had recently been sharpened on a whetstone.
Absalom crept around the estate, sword in hand, looking for this interloper.
He was quite drunk at the time, and it didn't take much effort to stumble his way into a field and see the man standing there. He was dressed in tattered gray rags, and his hands were outstretched and raised to the moon overhead.
With his eyes firmly heavenward, the seemingly insane man was babbling something, his voice hoarse and scratchy.
Absalom approached, his sword out, ready to strike at his first opening.
That man noticed Absalom, turned, and smiled at him. Without warning, Absalom struck the first blow. This alone should've killed him.
Instead, his gashing wound...it began to heal before Absalom's wide eyes. No blood seeped through the flesh, the leathery skin pulled itself together, and the wound healed over in a matter of seconds.
"What...art...thou?"
That man bore his fangs and leaped on Absalom. Despite looking so old, frail, and weak, he had the strength of a 1000 knights.
He pinned Absalom down and bit into his throat. That man...is Absalom's Sire. After the Embrace, the man left the land and was never seen again.
Absalom himself awakened in a field. In the post-Embrace Frenzy, he had killed at least a dozen peasants, and some of the nearby villagers fled in horror.
This, for the peasants, was the straw that broke the camel's back. Absalom's tyranny and ruthlessness to his subjects was at an all-time high, and many were gathering torches and sharpening pitchforks.
From the beginning of his Unlife, Absalom had quite the headstart in the Auspex Discipline. He could sense danger even before it came knocking.
The then-Fledgling felt something was coming, and he knew what was going to happen next. He packed some stuff into a sack, abandoned his family, and ran away from his own vast lands into the woods.
His head was a mess (still is). He barely even recalls his own name, and he could not remember how he became Kindred.
In time, most of his memories returned. It took years, and some of the details are fuzzy, but they've trickled back.
His Sire had given him the means and opportunity to escape his mortal doom. It was a kind, merciful gift, if anything. That roaming, roving Madman would go on to Sire more Childer in lands far and near.
Absalom has been through the Madness Network many times, and it's the only thing that's kept him from going completely insane (paradoxically, it's also nearly driven him nuts). He's made his peace with it, regardless.
Madeline's voice cuts through the shroud of Absalom's memories, "Hey. Hi. How are ya?"
"I am...I am fine, Madeline. How do you fare?"
"Everything's green on my end. Optimal, running, and stable. It's always nice and clean. How are you holding up, Absalom? Are you still there?"
Absalom's eyes slowly open, and he blinks a few times, "Yes. It takes time to...come around."
"Yeah. So, about what we discussed last night..."
The Elder groans, "Yes. You will help us to defeat the foe that has beset us? You will...ask the God-Machine?"
Madeline weakly chuckles, "I'm just a node in the God-Machine. I'll have to go through proper channels, send a few prayer signals up, and the like. It'll take a few cycles or so. But yes, I can absolutely give you a hand. If the God-Machine wills it, of course."
"Thank you. I don't know what else to say. What I have witnessed in regard to the God-Machine continues to...confound me. Is there anything else you wish to add?"
Madeline's synthetic voice cracks and signs of wistfulness and grief bleed through, "I ca--can't believe it's only been a decade since my--my assimilation. It feels like centuries, heh. I feel so...odd."
"Yes. What happened to you in Flimont City was...tragic. But you got vengeance on those who destroyed you, yes?"
Madeline ignores that and states, "We have to stop them, Absalom. The Second Inquisition, whoever they are in your part of the world. They're bad news. Everywhere."
Absalom sighs, "We shall do all we can possibly do, Madeline."
Some machine-like noises begin to fill the air. Madeline starts mumbling, "Optimizations required at Sector Theta-45. Dispatching. Optimizing. Logged. Store. Acceptable."
The Elder remarks, "Such...complexity."
Madeline goes on, "Infrastructure not found in current operating point. Insufficient resources to expand. Interest in expanding = negligible."
The Elder inquires, "Why can the God-Machine not make its presence known here?"
Madeline flatly answers, "It doesn't want to. There were a few Linchpins back in the 1990s, but when the UAE started growing, those same Linchpins started to...vanish."
"I see. What is this Linchpin that you speak of?"
Madeline shakily asks someone beyond Absalom's reach, "Ma--Master? Seeking permission to share limited info with entity? Entity is named Absalom. Like my former self, a Malkavian. Understood. Thank you."
"..."
Madeline resumes, "Permission granted. The God-Machine has deemed you worthy of access to certain data streams."
"That is most kind of it. Please relay my thanks."
"Access granted. You are welcome. All of the God-Machine's Infrastructure has a single, unremovable weak spot- a Linchpin. In the UAE, this was a prototype android who gained sentience/freewill and had to be destroyed/discarded."
"Excuse me? What are these androids you speak of? Is it like the phone operating system?"
Madeline's chuckle comes off as rather robotic and hollow, "No. It was a person who had undergone the process of Rewiring. Their consciousness was uploaded to the Infinite Matrix of the God-Machine."
Absalom is dumbfounded, "You are losing me, Madeline. Even for an Elder like myself, this is all far too much."
Madeline comes out and says it, "Androids are real, Absalom. They often call themselves the Rewired/Inorganic, just like vampires call themselves Kindred/Cainites. You got the Embrace to make new vamps and androids go through this whole process called Rewiring."
"How does that even work?"
Madeline makes a painful, strained noise, "Agrh. I--I can't divulge that. But the Rewired are out there, there are even some in your immediate location. Like the Masquerade, the Firewall protects them...provided they uphold/defend it."
Absalom dryly remarks, "How very intriguing. And you say that the consciousness of all of these...robots is inside the God-Machine?"
"That is...1 way to put it. Yes. Also, robots is a bit reductive. The Rewired have bodies of flesh, but their insides are...well...wires, circuits, chips, all that. They go to sleep while recharging themselves."
Absalom's eyebrow twitches, "They can be...defeated, can they?"
Madeline goes on, "Well, if you're talking about their physical forms, sure. But their consciousness is always safe/secure in the God-Machine. If anything happens to the shell/body, then a new one will be assigned to them. It's a pretty huge waiting list, heh."
"Can Kindred become androids? Can they go through this Rewiring process you speak of?"
"It depends. Why? Y--you wanna go through it?"
Absalom replies, "Why did you not go through with it? Or are you still on this waiting list?"
Madeline's voice quivers, "I--I have a different purpose within the God-Machine. It chose me all those years ago because of my...unique relationship with numbers. Numbers, equations, algorithms, data...all that."
"You are merely a node in a machine of infinite complexity. You are...happy with this? Truly?"
Madeline's reply is a whisper, "Yes. And I wanna to help you out, Absalom. So, yeah. I'm happy, or as...happy as can be, I guess. You don't know what it was like...looking back..." she trails off. She wishes to say more, but she does not.
Absalom sighs and picks up on a few things, "Very well. Let us discuss strategy."
Madeline whispers, "Absalom?"
"Yes?"
"Something's come up and it requires my immediate attention. I really have to take this. Oh, by the way, tell Mr. Bean I said hi. OK. We'll try to make contact later and discuss strategies. Logging. Store."
A high-pitched noise screeches across the air and the Elder Seer jolts awake. His mind is still reeling from learning about the existence of androids.
Over the course of his long Unlife, the Malkavian Primogen has encountered Garou, Mages, a few Wraiths, and fewer Changelings. He's even heard rumors of aliens being real.
This world is truly a strange and magical place. And a pretty dark one, too. Full of secrets, mysteries waiting to be unraveled.
Absalom rises from his day-sleep and feels his entire body shake as a shiver runs down the entirety of his cold, cold body.
His eyes dart around, and he looks over to the side, where a glass is filled with ruby-red liquid.
Mr. Bean left a handwritten note under it that reads: "Last of our supply. Kept it 4 u. Heading out for a walk. Have fun :-)"
Absalom gulps down the liquid and feels a rush of blood pumping into his Unliving body. His fingers tingle and his Beast rumbles in contentment.
With a shaky hand, he takes a look at his face in a nearby mirror. Still him, still around, still Absalom, still here.
He only came to the UAE back in 1970s.
When he was in Chicago, Absalom was quite good at running a Blood Bank. His super Auspex also makes him quite adept at planning and optimizing routes for the Blood Trade (amongst other things).
When he was driven here a few weeks back, the Elder helped to optimize a few of the paths of the Circulatory System in this part of the world.

For reasons obvious, this shit is illegal since it involves trafficking of human beings whose blood might have that certain tang to it, that peculiar taste, that sugary kick, a certain sensual saltiness, that...Resonance.
So, yeah, Absalom's been involved in all of that for quite a while. Around these parts, the Ministry has a monopoly on the sordid Blood Trade. As of late, the Tremere in Abu Dhabi are the #1 customers.
Absalom takes a bath, brushes his teeth, and walks around his penthouse. It's lacking in furnishings. The A/C makes this place feel so cool, though.
Almost too cool, but Absalom likes it that way. The Elder Malk plants his butt on the blue sofa and throws his head back.
Not even a minute goes by when the doorbell rings. Absalom groans and rises from his seat. The sound of the doorbell is annoying as hell. It's like a sharp knife stabbing into his ears.
Mr. Bean insisted on getting something that sounded crazier, but Absalom put his foot down.

Seth's smirk widens into a full-on toothy grin, "Now we're talking. Yeah, you tell this God-Machine I'm down to play ball."
The lights violently flicker and come on at full intensity. The A/C gets louder. The sudden intensity of the lights causes Seth's eyes to widen. Pain begins to shoot across his head, and his fangs are borne.
His skin itches and his chest aches. His eyes begin to bleed, and the light burns into his retinas. He feels sick.

The High Priest groans, "Ugghh..." he can feel the dimming lights eating into his eyes and making the pain even worse.
Absalom's voice booms throughout the penthouse apartment, "Madeline! Are you there? It's Absalom. Do not do that again. The Setites Abhor the Light. Or..."
Something dawns on the Elder. When she was still around, Madeline wouldn't have possibly known about all the Clans, their Banes, Compulsions, etc. But now that she is 1 with the God-Machine...
Absalom's mouth is a thin line, and his jaw tenses up, "Ah. Of course."
