Chapter 30: Familiar Faces- Part 1
"All around me are familiar faces / Worn out places, worn out faces" -Mad World (1982)
It is that time when the sun scorches the sky, the streets, and everything (and everybody) else. It's that time when the city of Al-Kushraf seems to come alive.
(Not like it ever sleeps, mind you).
During the morning time, the warm-bodied mortals (and a few Ghouls) go about their usual bidness. They buy stuff, they get lunch, they do what they gotta do to survive in this expensive-ass city.
Those who are in the know are pretty spooked by what happened the previous night...and the nights before. Many Setite Ghouls are understandably terrified- some of them don't even wanna go out in the open during daylight.
Once that great and terrible star of light, fire, and heat sets, the Kindred in this part of the world stir from their day-sleep.
Zeina Al-Qutub awakens when the alarm clock on her side table goes off. She smacks the snooze button and burrows herself deeper under the covers.
A few minutes later, she groggily sits up. The Assamite's Haven is a fairly modest and small 1-BHK apartment. It's not too fancy, but it's serviceable enough for her most immediate needs.
She runs her cold hands through her hair and thinks back to her office job in Dubai- it certainly paid better than her current job as a waitress in some hipster restaurant.
Like many in this city, Zeina is just struggling to get by. The tips are generous enough (mostly), but the unwanted comments, the random gropes from pervy customers, and the endless stream of assholes who think that they can get away with anything is exhausting.
Add to that the fact that she is out there- alongside her fellow Banu Haqim- fighting the Israeli menace and things are getting rather frustrating. She has to balance her work with her other obligations.
Last night, she and her fellow Assassins fought off a few small squads of SI. A few injuries were incurred by the vamps, but those Zionist assholes will be going back in bags.
Zeina is starting to get good at leading from the front. However, she remains unsure of how long this can keep going on. As a Banu Haqim, she has a Clan Compulsion to pass Judgment on her foes and a strong, deep-rooted desire to see her comrades succeed as well.
She also has an Unliving to make. Before heading for the shower, she grabs her touchscreen phone and checks if those jobs that she applied for have panned out.
Well, there's a promising one on the list. She makes a note to herself to contact the company and then proceeds to her bathroom. She certainly has the qualifications for the job in question.
During the late 1990s- after completing her higher studies in Iran- she was Embraced into the Blade of Haqim. She's been steadfastly faithful to the cause ever since.
On her phone, Zeina wistfully looks at the pictures of her relatives, many of whom are still in Lebanon. Every day, before day-sleeping, she makes sure to pray for their safety and wellbeing, especially from those goddamn bloodthirsty Israeli lunatics.
As it is, there is some unaddressed trauma in her past- that's clear. But it's been years since she was last visited by any kind of traumatic memories.
Her parents themselves were survivors of the 1982 Sabra and Shatila massacre, so she can't really imagine how awful it must've been to be in the middle of all that blood and violence.
(That's probably 1 of the reasons why they sent her off to Iran as soon as they could; the pain, the suffering, the never-ending torment is not something that many are capable of enduring).
Despite being an immortal being of the dark nights, she isn't above that as well. Furthermore, being a Banu Haqim is not without its disadvantages.
Back in Dubai, she committed a few things that would easily warrant the penalty of Final Death. Diablerie, in particular.
The Banu Haqim's Clan Bane is their Blood Addiction. They just gotta commit the big-D, no matter how much gasping or pearl-clutching or objections are involved.
And Zeina had no qualms about doing such a thing. In secret, she Diablerized a few Gangrel who were close to Prince Angela. Till this very night, nobody even suspects that it was her.
Well, there wasn't much to go off of besides piles of ashes and people not showing up or answering their emails or what have you.
In the mirror, Zeina sees her unkempt, messy hair, and she makes a face. She looks down and sees how black her veins are- thick with the (most obvious) sign of Diablerie.
She gulps and uneasily grimaces at her reflection. She knows that she's a vampire. She also knows that she is a Banu Haqim, of the Blade of Haqim.
Yet, she is also a Diablerist. She convinces herself that the people whom she drained deserved it.
"They were unjust. It is not my fault that I--I am like this. Other Clans have far worse Banes, walla! Far worse!"
Zeina licks her lips and trudges to the small bathroom where she slips out of her clothing, brushes her teeth (fangs, too!), and steps under the showerhead.
The warm water hits her cold, naked body, and she lets out a deep sigh. She tries to relax, but it's hard for her to let her guard down. She's always (ALWAYS) had her guard up and has rarely (if ever) allowed herself to let her guard down.
Her eyes dart all around the bathroom as her hands roam her body. Once done with her brief act of cleanliness, she wraps a towel around her wet body.
"I must be careful. I have to make sure that I'm not being followed or--or watched. I hope and pray that John has a plan to end this, and soon. Until then, I will do what I must to fight these accursed Zionist kilab (AR: dogs) and drive them away from these lands."
Back in the bedroom, the Assamite grabs the first set of clothes that she sees- a basic blouse and some loose-fitting slacks that look completely worn out.
"If only I had any other options to choose from...I would look for something more fitting and presentable. But this was only what I could grab when we were attacked and driven out of Dubai."
She bitterly scoffs as she combs her hair back and does her usual routine of applying a little makeup. From there, Ms. Al-Qutub proceeds to spray some vanilla-scented perfume on herself.
Zeina has had this bottle in her possession for the past 2 years now. She barely uses it and tries her best to not go overboard with it. She feels a little silly whenever she puts some of the sweet-smelling liquid on her dead skin, but it does help her feel...human.
As far as material possessions go, Zeina doesn't have much right now. She HAD quite a bit of stuff (mainly mementos from Lebanon, albums worth of photos, and even some random trinkets that belonged to her father), but she had to leave it behind when the raid on Dubai happened.
(She couldn't exactly take a whole Haven's worth of stuff with her when the time came).
Zeina does her best to keep in touch with her extended family back in Lebanon. Some are still living there whilst others have immigrated to the USA, the UK, and wherever they might find better prospects.
Some of her mortal family cousins ask her for pictures, but she politely declines because her looking the exact same as she did back in the 1990s is certainly bound to raise a few eyebrows.
The Masquerade isn't just something to be taken lightly. It's something to be respected. Even the most seasoned vampires like John and the others usually don't want to be caught breaking the Masquerade.
Even if it is (temporarily) shattered in this Domain, its importance for the Kindred of this region cannot be taken lightly. As long as those malicious Orgs are out there, the Masquerade is a necessary matter of survival.
That is why Zeina Al-Qutub takes all of these matters very seriously. She was only all the gladder to be Embraced. For her, it's a way to get back at the fascist structures that played a role in the massacring of her people- and all the other innocents.
Zeina knows that her own existence is a small part of the whole, but she's ready to do what it takes to make sure that justice is served, no matter the cost.
Then again, she is a human being like the rest of us. She has bills to pay, a roof to maintain over her head, and a whole lot of other things that she'd like to do with her free time, which she doesn't get much of due to a variety of reasons.
But the Judge does sincerely try her absolute best to be a good, upstanding person. She tries to be kind, understanding, and considerate to all the people (be they mortal or immortal) around her, especially those who are in similar circumstances.
(There's plenty of 'em at work!)
Speaking of, Zeina gives herself a once-over and prepares to head out for yet another shift at that midrange hipster joint. On the bus, she silently prays that tonight will go smoothly.
Before entering the restaurant proper, she circles around back and quickly sticks her pointy fangs in some random stranger. Without killing them, she takes only what is needed to keep the eternal Beast sated and at bay.
After licking the wounds shut, she then lays the mortal's still-breathing body on the rough floor and does her best to look innocent as she walks into the kitchen.
She's immediately hit with the smell of juicy meats, sautéed vegetables, and other assorted yummies which she is no longer capable of partaking of. A few mortal chefs are busy cooking and chopping away, their arms moving at a steady pace.
The sweaty, scruffy mortals are clad in loose fitting outfits- aprons and chef hats, the whole culinary shebang. Zeina grabs her apron and gets busy.
She's not exactly thrilled about tonight's schedule, but she knows that there isn't much of an alternative. That's what she keeps telling herself, anyway.
As she's serving food or clearing up empty (or half-empty) plates, she ponders upon her future prospects and tries to keep her mind occupied with the tasks at hand.
Such is Unlife for a Kindred of her stature. It could certainly be better. But then again, it could always be worse, too. And if there's anything she's learned over these years, it's that she's gotta make do with what she's got.
She's not one to complain too much, not really. Not when there are people who are far worse off than she is.
John is all up and awake now. His gaping wound looks like it's all closed up. Right now, he's just coming back from teaching Kenpo to some expat types who just happen to be staying in the same apartment building.
His legs take him down the steps and out into the wider world. The night is still young, and the city is still buzzing. John isn't too keen on doing much, but he's never been one for stillness.
He already slaked his Hunger soon after waking up- there was a drunk fella who wasn't quite all there. He was easy pickings. John made sure to lick the wounds closed and leave the (still-breathing) body by the service door.
For now, Mr. Suarez hails a taxi and sits back while being driven to the location for his next teaching gig. During the ride, he shoves his earphones deep into his ears.
He scrolls through his music playlist and smirks when Kristin Mainhart's velvety voice comes through. Oh, he absolutely loves her singing.
While stuck in traffic, the Assamite Primogen looks around and sees that the city is as bustling as ever. Orange streetlights coat the cityscape and reflect off the many buildings.
All the stores are packed full- the restaurants, the pubs, the bars...and so on. Throngs of patrons can be seen lining up as a gigantic-looking bouncer feller checks IDs and nods approvingly.
John takes out a single earphone, and his ears are immediately drawn to a loud and noisy sound coming from behind him. It sounds like the revving of a bullet bike's engine.
He narrows his eyes and looks out the back window. A man in a motorcycle helmet and black leather jacket is revving his engine and getting a little rowdy.
He presses his horn and keeps on revving and revving. John's brows knit together. He tells the taxi driver to pull over to the side, but the taxi driver says that the light has just turn green.
John clicks his tongue and hops out of the car. He slowly walks towards the man on the bike, feeling that he recognizes who might be under that helmet.
The guy in the helmet looks down at his phone and doesn't see the Assamite coming.
John cautiously approaches and cautiously asks, "Ravinder?"
The man on the bullet bike looks up and sees that it's John. He takes off his helmet and grins, "Well, well, well. Arey! John!"
He kills the engine and pulls the Assamite in for a tight hug. They pat each other's backs, and the 2 men chuckle a bit.
Ravinder Rao is of Clan Ravnos. He is in his 40s, has dark, almost ashy skin. He's got a somewhat medium build. His eyes are a light shade of brown, and his dark hair appears to be quite short.
He is wearing a plain gray shirt, some denim jeans, and a leather jacket. His full-face helmet is opaque and black.
He speaks with an Indian (as in the subcontinent) accent when he jovially asks, "What the bloody hell are you doing here, man?"
The taxi driver sticks his head out and looks a little frustrated when he asks if John is gonna get back in so this ride can continue. John shakes his head and tells him to wait.
Ravinder grins widely and looks at his old friend from head to toe, "It has been too long, my friend. Too long. I heard about what happened in Dubai. I am sorry. I did not know that things had gotten so...bad."
John clicks his tongue and twitches slightly, "Yeah. We had no other choice but to run like hell when all that shit went down, man. We were originally meant to go to Abu Dhabi, but the Tremere didn't want any Banu Haqim in their Domain, so Al-Kushraf is where we ended up."
Ravinder's smile falters, and he looks a little concerned, "I have been keeping myself updated with the news. I suppose you should be thankful that there was somebody to take you in, right?"
John shrugs, "Eh. The Ministry's got some stuff going on here, but that's not something I'm really bothered with right now. How the hell have you been. We uh...saw each other, what...4 years ago?"
The Raven bites his lower lip, "Mm. Yes. That is right. What a journey it has been since then, truly!"
The Ravnos are perpetually Doomed (it's their Clan's Bane). If they stay in a single place for too long, the very same sun fire that incinerated their founder will be waiting for them, ready to tear them from the inside out.
So, after getting Embraced somewhere in the early 1990s, the Raven elaborately quit his dead-end IT job in Dubai by telling his boss to go and fuck herself. He took out all of his lifesavings, bought a bullet bike, and hasn't looked back ever since.
Ravinder goes into how he has ridden all the way to Bangladesh, met with the few surviving Elders of his Clan, and has been perpetually on the move.
"I rode with quite a Bande, my friend! Quite a Bande!! We were going at speeds unimaginable! We did things that truly made us worth of the 'Daredevil' moniker."
He pauses and stares off into the far distance, "Ah, it was...quite the high, John. Quite the high."
John and Ravinder had been friends back in Dubai, but Ravinder's peripatetic Unlifestyle meant that the 2 could not really meet that often, let alone keep in touch.
The Wanderer's story is pretty well-known- he's the classic "rider, traveler, wanderer" who's always on the go and never in a single location for too damn long, lest his Blood scorch him alive.
The Assamite can't help but smile when he sees the wistful expression on the Ravnos's face, "That's...I'm happy for ya, man. You saw a good chunk of the world, had some fun, made some memories, met a bunch of interesting folks along the way."
Ravinder chuckles, "That I did. And you, my friend? Your Childe, is she still with us?"
"Yep. Svetlana's doing fine. I know that it hasn't been long for her, but she's adjusting well and doing her part in the struggle against the SI. Those bastards have their claws deep in this region, so yeah- it's pretty rough around here."
"I can imagine. If I could, I would summon my old Bande to help you destroy these bloody SI chodes. Oh, you won't believe the kinds of people who tried to hunt us down, John!"
John quirks an eyebrow, "Oh?"
The taxi driver gets annoyed, throws his arms up, and peels off. He accepts another fare, shakes his head at John, and speeds away.
John turns his head and frowns. Ravinder pats his friend's shoulder, "Ah, do not worry. I'll give you a ride to wherever you wish to go, habibi. It is no problem."
He clears his throat, "Now, as I was saying. My old Bande was quite diverse, arey! We had Elders and Fledglings alike. We had some who were quite gifted in the arts- you know, musicians and poets and the like. We also..."
Ravinder stops to laugh and goes on, "Hahahahaha! We also had people who could not tell the difference between a wrench and a spanner! Some of them couldn't even change a tire if their Unlives depended on it! But oh, did they enjoy the thrills of riding all the same!"
John smiles, "That's great. Are you um...um...sure about taking me to wherever I wanna go? I know that you're in the middle of some big road trip right now, and I don't want to keep you from that."
Ravinder clicks his tongue, "Arey, John. Still the same, eh? Even after all these nights. I think I can afford to stay in this city for a few days before I have to move again. And how is my good friend...Abdullah."
John's undead heart is stung when that name is brought up. He lowers his head pensively, "He's...he's gone."
"Hmm?"
"The SI destroyed him and they made a video of the whole thing. It--it was the worst thing that I--I ever saw. Those bastards left him out for the sun. But--but they tortured him before leaving him out. Abdullah's gone, man. He's gone."
The Ravnos grimaces and covers his mouth with his gloved hand, "Oh my god. I--I am so sorry, John." He reaches out and touches the Assamite's arm, "That--that is just the worst thing that I have ever heard."
John nods slightly, "Yeah. It is. And now, we're fighting back...with everything and--and everyone that we've got. I'll tell you more on the way."
Ravinder hands his Banu Haqim pal an extra helmet and tells him to hop on the back of his bike. The Assamite does so, and they speed off into the night.
The Assamite Primogen does his best to bring the Daredevil up to speed on what's been going on for the past few weeks in Al-Kushraf. Ravinder oohs and aahs and exclaims in surprise at some parts.
He is genuinely saddened and disgusted when he learns of the fate that had befallen Abdullah Rachman and the other Assamites who've been bravely fighting against the Israeli menace.

Ravinder's shiny bullet bike whooshes through the traffic, and the 2 friends are back to chatting again.

Always on the move, always Tempting Fate (the Ravnos Clan Compulsion). And Ravinder here has certainly done his fair share of that over the years!

It'd be hard, really hard. But he'd get it done. He thinks so. Maybe. Would he?
For now, Mr. Suarez puts these queries to the back of his mind and enjoys the cool breeze that comes with being on the road at these insane speeds.
